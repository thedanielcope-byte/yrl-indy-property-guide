For many buyers over 55, the goal shifts from more square footage to less upkeep. Active adult communities across the Indianapolis metro answer that with single-level homes, main-floor primary suites, and HOAs that handle the lawn and the snow so weekends stay free. These neighborhoods come in two flavors: age-restricted communities that legally limit residency to households 55 and older, and age-targeted communities that welcome all ages but are designed around ranch and villa floor plans that appeal to downsizers. Many include a clubhouse, fitness room, walking trails, and an active social calendar. Where to Find 55+ Communities in Central Indiana

Active adult and low-maintenance neighborhoods are spread across the suburbs, with new ones opening every year. Hamilton County leads the way — Fishers, Westfield, and Noblesville all feature builder-led villa and ranch communities with clubhouses and maintained grounds. On the south side, Greenwood offers a strong mix of attached villas and single-level patio homes at approachable prices. West of Indianapolis, Avon and Brownsburg continue to add low-maintenance neighborhoods near shopping and healthcare. For a wider look at the market these communities sit within, browse our Indianapolis homes for sale hub or our dedicated ranch homes guide.

55+ Community Home Prices

Pricing in active adult communities reflects the home style and the level of amenities. Attached villas and smaller ranch homes typically start in the $250s, offering an efficient, lock-and-leave lifestyle at a manageable price. The heart of the market — freestanding ranch and patio homes with two or three bedrooms, a main-floor primary, and a two-car garage — generally falls in the $300s to $400s. Larger custom patio homes in higher-end, amenity-rich communities can reach the $500s. Most of these neighborhoods carry a monthly HOA fee that covers lawn care, snow removal, and shared amenities, so it's worth weighing that cost against the convenience it buys. A market analysis helps you compare true monthly cost across communities.

Who 55+ Communities Are Right For

These communities are built for people entering a lower-maintenance chapter — empty nesters ready to trade yard work for travel, retirees who want everything on one level, and buyers thinking ahead about accessibility and aging in place. Single-level floor plans with wider doorways and step-free entries make daily life easier now and years from now. The social side matters too: many buyers choose an active adult community specifically for the built-in community — neighbors at a similar stage, planned events, and shared amenities. It's also a smart fit for anyone who wants to own without the burden of exterior upkeep. If that describes your next move, the right community depends on your budget, preferred location, and how much amenity you actually want.

Frequently Asked Questions — 55+ Communities

What is a 55+ active adult community?

A 55+ community is a neighborhood designed for adults age 55 and older. Age-restricted communities legally limit residency by age, while age-targeted communities are simply designed to appeal to that buyer with low-maintenance, single-level homes. Both typically feature lawn and snow care, ranch or villa floor plans, and shared amenities like clubhouses and walking paths.

How much do 55+ homes cost in the Indianapolis area?

Homes in 55+ communities generally range from the $250s for attached villas and smaller ranch homes to the $500s for larger custom patio homes in higher-end communities. Most fall in the $300s to $400s. Monthly HOA fees that cover exterior maintenance are common. Contact Your Realty Link for a free market analysis.

Where are the best 55+ communities near Indianapolis?

Active adult and low-maintenance communities are found throughout the metro, with strong concentrations in Fishers, Westfield, Noblesville, Greenwood, Avon, and Brownsburg, as well as on the south side near Southport. New builder-led 55+ neighborhoods continue to open in the growth suburbs.
