Hero Photo Checklist

Indy Property Guide · indypropertyguide.com  |  Check these off as you collect them. Send in any order — pages without a photo keep the current navy gradient, so nothing breaks.

Specs

  • Landscape only — never portrait
  • 1600px wide minimum (2400px+ better)
  • Roughly 3:2 or wider
  • Send originals — don't compress first
  • JPEG / PNG / HEIC all fine

Composition — matters more than resolution

  • Keep the left third simple (sky, lawn, water) — the headline sits there
  • Put the subject on the right
  • Shoot wide — the hero is a short, wide band
  • Golden hour beats overcast midday every time
  • Avoid faces, license plates, other brokerages' signs
If you only send five: Homepage, Carmel, Fishers, Greenwood, and the downtown Indianapolis skyline. That covers the highest-traffic pages and the site will already feel transformed.

⚠ Before you use listing photos: don't reuse MLS listing photography unless you own the rights. It's normally the photographer's or listing brokerage's copyright, and MLS rules restrict reuse. Your own listings, shot by your own photographer with rights assigned, are fine.
Real local landmarks beat stock houses. A photo of the actual Carmel Arts & Design District beats ten stock photos of an anonymous suburban house — it's the single biggest thing that stops the site looking like every other agent template.
How to send: drop everything in one folder (any filenames — the names above are just for reference) and say where it is. Each photo gets resized, EXIF-stripped, converted to WebP + JPEG, and wired to its page automatically.
