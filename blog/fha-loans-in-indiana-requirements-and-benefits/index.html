FHA Loans in Indiana — Requirements and Benefits

FHA loans are one of the most accessible paths to homeownership in Indiana. Here's everything you need to know about credit requirements, down payments, mortgage insurance, and how to get started.

💰 Mortgage & Finance 🏡 Indianapolis & Central Indiana ✍️ By Daniel Cope
Mortgage & Finance July 2026 By Daniel Cope, Real Estate Broker — Your Realty Link ~9 min read

If you're thinking about buying a home in Indiana but you're worried about your credit score, your savings, or qualifying for a traditional mortgage, you're not alone — and an FHA loan might be exactly the solution you need. FHA loans are backed by the Federal Housing Administration, which means lenders can offer more flexible qualification requirements than conventional mortgages. They've been helping Americans become homeowners for decades, and they remain one of the most popular loan programs in the Indianapolis market — especially for first-time buyers, buyers rebuilding their credit, and anyone who doesn't have a large down payment saved up. In this guide, we'll break down the FHA loan requirements in Indiana, explain how FHA compares to conventional financing, and walk you through how to get started.

What Is an FHA Loan?

An FHA loan is a mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration, a division of the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). The FHA doesn't actually lend you money — it insures the loan, which means it guarantees the lender against loss if you default. This government backing is what allows lenders to offer more favorable terms to borrowers who might not qualify for a conventional mortgage.

FHA loans have been around since 1934, and they've helped millions of Americans buy homes — particularly first-time buyers and those with modest incomes or less-than-perfect credit. In Indiana, FHA loans are used widely across the Indianapolis metro and throughout Central Indiana, from Carmel and Fishers to Greenwood, Avon, and beyond.

The key advantages of FHA loans include lower down payment requirements, more flexible credit score thresholds, and the ability to use gift funds for your down payment and closing costs. These features make FHA an especially attractive option in an affordable market like Indianapolis, where home prices are within reach for many buyers who just need a little more flexibility in financing. Credit Score Requirements for FHA Loans

One of the biggest advantages of FHA financing is the lower credit score threshold compared to conventional loans. Here's how it works:

  • 580 or higher: You qualify for the minimum down payment of 3.5%. This is the sweet spot for most FHA borrowers in Indiana.
  • 500 to 579: You can still qualify for an FHA loan, but you'll need a larger down payment — typically 10% of the purchase price.
  • Below 500: Most lenders will not approve an FHA loan at this credit score level.

It's worth noting that the FHA sets minimum guidelines, but individual lenders can (and often do) set higher standards. Some lenders require a minimum score of 620 or 640 even for FHA loans. This is called a "lender overlay." If one lender turns you down, it doesn't mean FHA is off the table — another lender with different overlays may approve you. A good real estate agent can connect you with FHA-friendly lenders in the Indianapolis area who work regularly with buyers at various credit levels.

Down Payment: Just 3.5% — and Gift Funds Are Allowed

The FHA's 3.5% minimum down payment is one of its most compelling features. On a $250,000 home in Indianapolis — a very realistic price point in communities like Greenwood, Avon, or Plainfield — that's just $8,750 down. Compare that to a conventional loan, which typically requires 5% to 20% down, and the math is clear: FHA makes homeownership accessible sooner.

Even better, FHA allows 100% of your down payment to come from gift funds — meaning a family member, employer, or approved charitable organization can give you the money for your down payment without it affecting your loan qualification. The gift must be properly documented (your lender will walk you through this), but it's a legitimate and commonly used strategy.

Indiana also has several down payment assistance programs that can be layered on top of an FHA loan. The Indiana Housing and Community Development Authority (IHCDA) offers programs that provide down payment and closing cost assistance to qualifying first-time buyers. Understanding FHA Mortgage Insurance (MIP)

FHA loans require mortgage insurance — this is how the FHA funds its guarantee to lenders. There are two components:

Upfront Mortgage Insurance Premium (UFMIP): This is a one-time fee charged at closing, currently set at 1.75% of the loan amount. On a $240,000 loan (after a 3.5% down payment on a $250,000 home), that's approximately $4,200. The good news is that this fee is almost always rolled into your loan balance — you don't pay it out of pocket at closing.

Annual Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP): This is an ongoing monthly charge that's included in your mortgage payment. The rate varies based on your loan term, loan amount, and down payment, but for most Indianapolis buyers it falls in the range of 0.50% to 0.85% of the loan balance per year. On that $240,000 loan, annual MIP might run roughly $1,200 to $2,000 per year, or $100 to $170 per month added to your payment.

One important difference between FHA MIP and conventional private mortgage insurance (PMI): for most FHA loans originated with less than 10% down, MIP stays on the loan for its entire life. With a conventional loan, you can typically drop PMI once you reach 20% equity. This is one of the trade-offs of FHA financing — and it's a reason some buyers eventually refinance from FHA to conventional once their credit improves and they've built sufficient equity.

FHA Property Requirements in Indiana

FHA loans aren't just about the borrower — the property itself has to meet certain standards. The FHA wants to make sure it's insuring a loan on a safe, habitable home that will hold its value. Here's what the FHA appraisal looks for:

  • Safety: The home must be structurally sound with no health or safety hazards. This includes functioning electrical and plumbing systems, a sound roof, no lead-based paint hazards (in homes built before 1978), and safe access and egress.
  • Habitability: The home must have adequate heating, a working kitchen, and a functional bathroom. It needs to be livable as-is — not a fixer-upper that requires major work before someone can move in.
  • Foundation and structure: Crawl spaces must be accessible and dry. Foundations can't show signs of major structural failure. Basements need to be free of standing water.
  • Roof condition: The roof must have at least two years of remaining useful life. Missing shingles, active leaks, or significant deterioration will flag the appraisal.

In the Indianapolis market, FHA property requirements occasionally knock out older homes that have deferred maintenance — particularly homes with older roofs, outdated electrical panels, or peeling exterior paint on pre-1978 construction. If you're buying with FHA, your agent should help you identify potential appraisal issues early, before you get too far into the process. FHA vs. Conventional Loans: Which Is Better?

This is one of the most common questions we hear from Indianapolis home buyers, and the honest answer is: it depends on your situation. Here's a quick comparison:

Choose FHA if:

  • Your credit score is below 700 (and especially below 680)
  • You have limited savings for a down payment
  • You have a higher debt-to-income ratio
  • You're a first-time buyer who needs flexibility

Choose conventional if:

  • Your credit score is 720 or higher
  • You can put 10% to 20% down
  • You want to avoid lifetime mortgage insurance
  • You're buying a higher-priced home that might exceed FHA limits

In practice, many Indianapolis buyers start with FHA because it gets them into a home sooner, then refinance to a conventional loan after a few years of building equity and improving their credit. This is a perfectly valid strategy — and one we see work well regularly.

Your lender should run the numbers both ways and show you the monthly payment comparison. If you're not sure where to start, Your Realty Link can connect you with lenders who specialize in both FHA and conventional financing in the Indianapolis market.

How to Get Started With an FHA Loan in Indianapolis

Ready to explore FHA financing? Here's the step-by-step process:

  1. Check your credit. Pull your free credit reports from AnnualCreditReport.com and review them for errors. Even small corrections can improve your score.
  2. Talk to a lender. Get pre-approved — not just pre-qualified — with an FHA-approved lender. Pre-approval tells you exactly how much you can borrow and signals to sellers that you're a serious buyer.
  3. Connect with an agent. Work with a buyer's agent who understands FHA-compatible properties. Your Realty Link agents know which Indianapolis neighborhoods and home types work well with FHA financing — and which ones will trigger appraisal issues.
  4. Start shopping. Search homes on yourrealtylink.com and let your agent know your FHA pre-approval amount, must-haves, and preferred areas.
  5. Make an offer and close. Your agent and lender will guide you through the offer, appraisal, inspection, and closing process. FHA closings typically take 30 to 45 days from accepted offer to keys in hand.
