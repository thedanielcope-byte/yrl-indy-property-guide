Jamestown Indiana Real Estate & Homes for Sale

📍 SW Boone County 🏫 Western Boone Schools 🏡 $150s – $280s+ ~50 min to Downtown Indy

Jamestown sits in southwestern Boone County along US-136, just a few miles north of I-74 and roughly 50 minutes northwest of downtown Indianapolis. With a town population of about 900 and a school district drawn from a broader rural area, Jamestown is one of the smallest towns in the Indianapolis metro footprint and one of the most genuinely rural — surrounded on every side by working farmland, woodlots, and quiet country roads. The town is anchored by its tiny but functional downtown along US-136 and Main Street, the surrounding agricultural economy, and the Western Boone Community Schools system that brings the local students together. For buyers seeking real rural living, accessible prices, and a school district that's part of the Western Boone tradition, Jamestown is one of Boone County's most affordable options. Living in Jamestown, Indiana

Life in Jamestown is genuinely rural. The town itself is small enough that you'll know most of your neighbors within a year, and the surrounding countryside provides quiet country roads, working farms, and the kind of wide-open Hoosier landscape that has become harder to find in the eastern part of the metro. US-136 runs through town, connecting Jamestown west toward Crawfordsville and east toward Lebanon, while I-74 sits just a few miles south for faster access to Indianapolis. The community calendar is small but meaningful — Friday-night Western Boone Stars athletics, local church and community events, and the kind of community where neighbors actually look out for each other. For buyers who want a genuinely slow pace, real rural property at attainable prices, and the option of country acreage and hobby-farm life, Jamestown delivers in a way few small towns in the metro can match.

Jamestown Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Jamestown market is one of the most affordable in all of Boone County and one of the more accessible rural markets within reasonable driving distance of Indianapolis. Homes typically range from the $150s for established single-family homes, starter properties, and homes in older parts of town to the $280s and beyond for larger homes, updated properties, and rural homes with acreage. The market is small and personal — inventory turns over slowly, and many transactions involve unique properties such as historic homes, working farms, hobby farms, or rural homes with substantial land. For first-time buyers, retirees, remote workers, and anyone willing to trade longer commute time for substantially lower housing costs and real country living, Jamestown represents some of Boone County's strongest value. For investors, the market is thin, so resale liquidity is a consideration — but well-located properties continue to attract steady tenant demand from families and workers tied to the local agricultural and small-business economy.

Schools in Jamestown, Indiana

Jamestown is served by the Western Boone Community School Corporation, which operates Western Boone Junior-Senior High School and feeder elementary schools throughout the district. The district is well regarded for its small class sizes, strong community involvement, and competitive athletic programs — particularly the Western Boone Stars, who consistently field strong basketball, football, and other athletic teams. Families relocating to western Boone County who want a small-school experience often land on Western Boone for exactly that reason. A Your Realty Link agent can help you confirm attendance details for any home you're considering.

Commute from Jamestown to Indianapolis

Jamestown's commute math is the longest of Boone County's incorporated communities, but workable for many buyers. Downtown Indianapolis is roughly 35 miles southeast — about a 50-minute drive via US-136 and I-74 to I-465. The closer Brownsburg and Avon employment corridor in Hendricks County is approximately 35 minutes south via I-74, putting major retailers, distribution centers, and the Hendricks County job market within a reasonable drive. Lebanon — Boone County's seat — is about 20 minutes northeast via US-136. For remote workers, hybrid workers, and anyone working in the broader western Indianapolis metro who values substantially lower housing costs and country living, Jamestown represents a real trade-off worth considering.
