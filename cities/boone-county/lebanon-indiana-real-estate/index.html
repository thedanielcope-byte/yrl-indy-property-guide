📍 Boone County 🏫 Lebanon Community Schools 🏡 $200s – $400s+ ~35 min to Downtown Indy

Lebanon is the seat of Boone County and its largest city, sitting along the I-65 corridor about 35 minutes northwest of downtown Indianapolis. With a population of roughly 17,000 and a steady pace of growth, Lebanon blends a genuine historic downtown — anchored by the architecturally striking Boone County Courthouse, restored brick storefronts, and locally owned businesses — with an expanding network of newer subdivisions on the city's edges. The city is home to Witham Health Services, the major regional hospital and one of the area's largest employers, along with a growing distribution and logistics corridor along I-65 that includes Eli Lilly's substantial LEAP innovation district investment just south of town. The combination of historic small-city character, strong schools, and direct interstate access to Indianapolis has driven steady demand for Lebanon homes. Living in Lebanon, Indiana

Lebanon's downtown square is one of the strongest in the broader Indianapolis area. The Boone County Courthouse — a massive limestone-and-granite building completed in 1911 — anchors a square ringed by locally owned restaurants, shops, and the historic Boone County Memorial Hall. Witham Memorial Park, Memorial Park, and Abner Longley Park provide green space and recreation throughout the city. The annual Boone County 4-H Fair and the Lebanon Independence Day celebration draw the broader community together each summer. Beyond the historic core, newer residential subdivisions along the I-65 corridor and SR-32/SR-39 have brought modern construction and amenities. The State Road 39 commercial corridor provides everyday shopping, and Lebanon's location along I-65 puts both Indianapolis and the broader Boone County employment base within easy reach. For buyers who want a real small-city downtown with full interstate access and strong schools, Lebanon is consistently one of Boone County's strongest options.

Lebanon Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Lebanon real estate market offers an unusually wide range of housing styles and price points. Homes typically range from the $200s for established single-family homes and historic homes in older neighborhoods near downtown to the $400s and beyond for newer construction and larger homes in newer subdivisions on the city's edges. Lebanon's historic neighborhoods near the courthouse square offer real architectural character — restored Victorians, Craftsman bungalows, and early 20th-century homes on tree-lined streets — while newer subdivisions along the I-65 corridor and the SR-39 corridor provide modern construction. The city's steady growth, anchored by Witham Health Services, the Eli Lilly LEAP innovation district, and the I-65 distribution corridor, has supported consistent demand across price points. Property values have appreciated steadily over the past decade, and continued employment growth points to further appreciation ahead.

Schools in Lebanon, Indiana

Lebanon is served by the Lebanon Community School Corporation, which operates Lebanon High School, Lebanon Middle School, and feeder elementary schools throughout the city. The district is well regarded for its academic, career-technical, and arts programming, and Lebanon High School fields strong athletic programs that draw enthusiastic community support. Families relocating to Boone County who want strong schools at attainable price points often land on Lebanon after their research. The district's broad footprint also includes some surrounding rural areas, so a Your Realty Link agent can help you understand which schools serve any home you're considering and recommend confirming current attendance details directly with the district.

Commute from Lebanon to Indianapolis

Lebanon commuters benefit from direct I-65 access at SR-32 and SR-39, with downtown Indianapolis approximately 25 miles south — about a 35-minute drive under normal conditions. The Hamilton County employment corridor (Carmel, Fishers) is approximately 30 minutes east via I-65 and I-865. The I-65 corridor between Lebanon and Indianapolis has also developed into a significant employment area, with major distribution facilities, the Eli Lilly LEAP innovation district at the AllPoints Midwest park (a substantial pharmaceutical manufacturing investment just south of Lebanon), and other major employers within minutes of Lebanon homes. For buyers prioritizing accessible Indianapolis and Hamilton County access without the higher housing costs of those areas, Lebanon consistently delivers strong commute and employment math.
