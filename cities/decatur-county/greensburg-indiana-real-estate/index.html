📍 Decatur County🌳 Famous Courthouse Tree🏡 $180s – $320s+~55 min to Downtown Indy

Greensburg is the seat of Decatur County and one of southeastern Indiana's most distinctive small cities. Sitting along the I-74 corridor about 55 minutes southeast of downtown Indianapolis, Greensburg has a population of roughly 12,000 and continues to grow steadily as a regional manufacturing and commercial hub. The city is best known nationally for a quirky civic landmark: a tree growing from the top of the Decatur County Courthouse tower — a phenomenon that has continued for over a century and given Greensburg the nickname "Tree City." The city's economy is anchored by the major Honda Manufacturing of Indiana plant just west of town (one of the largest auto manufacturing facilities in the Midwest) plus a strong network of suppliers and other employers along I-74. Combined with a charming historic downtown, strong schools, and affordable housing, Greensburg offers genuine value for buyers who can accommodate the commute. Living in Greensburg, Indiana

Life in Greensburg revolves around the historic downtown square anchored by the famous tree-topped courthouse. The downtown features restored brick buildings housing locally owned restaurants, breweries, shops, and the Decatur County Historical Society. The annual Tree City Fall Festival each fall draws visitors from across the region and is one of southeastern Indiana's longest-running community events. Beyond downtown, Rebekah Park, Greensburg City Park, and the city's network of smaller parks provide green space and family recreation. Friday-night Greensburg Pirates athletics and the steady community rhythm give the city a strong social calendar. The major employment along I-74 — Honda, Decatur Mold, and other manufacturers — provides a stable working-economy character that supports steady residential demand. For buyers prioritizing real small-city character, manufacturing employment, affordable housing, and accessible Indianapolis and Cincinnati access via I-74, Greensburg delivers a distinctive package.

Greensburg Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Greensburg real estate market offers strong value with an unusually wide mix of housing reflecting the city's size and steady manufacturing employment base. Homes typically range from the $180s for established single-family homes and historic homes near downtown to the $320s and beyond for newer construction and larger homes in newer subdivisions on the city's edges. The mix of housing — historic homes near the courthouse square, mid-century neighborhoods, newer subdivisions, and country properties on the surrounding farmland — gives buyers genuine variety. The Honda plant and supplier network bring steady workforce demand that supports the entry-level and mid-range market, while ongoing residential growth has brought modern construction at varied price points. For investors, Greensburg offers a workable combination of accessible acquisition prices and steady tenant demand from manufacturing workers and families tied to the school district.

Schools in Greensburg, Indiana

Greensburg is served by the Greensburg Community School Corporation, which operates Greensburg Community High School, Greensburg Junior High School, and feeder elementary schools. The district is well regarded for its academic, career-technical, and athletic programs, and the Greensburg Pirates field strong programs that draw enthusiastic community support. Families relocating to Decatur County for Honda or other I-74 corridor employment often land on Greensburg Community Schools after their research. A Your Realty Link agent can help you confirm attendance details for any home you're considering.

Commute from Greensburg to Indianapolis

Greensburg's commute math is longer than central Indianapolis-adjacent communities but workable for many buyers. Downtown Indianapolis is approximately 50 miles northwest — about a 55-minute drive via I-74 west to I-465, depending on traffic. Columbus, IN is approximately 30 minutes southwest via SR-46. Cincinnati is about 90 minutes east via I-74. For Honda employees, manufacturing workers, hybrid workers, and remote workers, Greensburg offers a meaningful trade-off: substantially lower housing costs than Indianapolis-adjacent communities in exchange for the longer drive.
