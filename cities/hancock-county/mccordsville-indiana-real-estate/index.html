McCordsville Indiana Real Estate & Homes for Sale

McCordsville is one of Indiana's fastest-growing communities. Your Realty Link's our agents help buyers and sellers navigate this rapidly expanding Hancock County market.

📍 Hancock County 🏫 Mt. Vernon Schools 🏡 $280s–$500s Near Geist Reservoir

McCordsville, Indiana has undergone one of the most remarkable transformations of any community in the Indianapolis metro over the past decade. What was once a small rural crossroads in western Hancock County has become one of Indiana's fastest-growing towns, driven by its proximity to Geist Reservoir, the Hamilton County border, and the I-69 employment corridor anchored by Fishers. Located roughly 20 to 25 miles northeast of downtown Indianapolis, McCordsville sits at a compelling intersection of location, value, and growth potential. Buyers who want newer construction at price points below adjacent Fishers or Carmel have discovered McCordsville as a compelling alternative — one where their dollar stretches further while their lifestyle doesn't have to. Your Realty Link serves buyers and sellers throughout McCordsville and Hancock County with full MIBOR MLS access and agents who understand the pace of this fast-moving market.

Living in McCordsville, Indiana

McCordsville's lifestyle appeal is tied closely to its natural setting. The proximity to Geist Reservoir — one of the Indianapolis metro's premier recreational waterways — gives residents access to boating, fishing, waterfront dining, and scenic trails that most suburban communities can only envy. As the town has grown, retail and restaurant options along SR-67 have expanded to serve a rapidly increasing residential base, with more development on the way. The community has the energy of a place that is actively becoming — new neighborhoods opening, new amenities arriving, new neighbors moving in — which creates a sense of shared investment and pride that established suburbs sometimes lose. For buyers who want to get into a growing market while it's still moving, McCordsville represents a genuine opportunity.

McCordsville Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The McCordsville real estate market is dominated by newer construction, which reflects the town's recent growth phase. Homes typically range from the $280s for entry-level new construction to the upper $500s for larger homes in premium neighborhoods with Geist-area proximity or upgraded finishes. The market compares favorably to adjacent Hamilton County communities where equivalent new construction frequently commands a significant premium. Buyers who purchase in McCordsville now are positioned well for appreciation as the community continues to develop infrastructure and commercial amenities that typically follow residential growth. Resale inventory is still limited given how recently much of the housing stock was built, which tends to support values in the near term.

Schools in McCordsville, Indiana

McCordsville is served by Mt. Vernon Community Schools, a Hancock County district that is itself growing alongside the communities it serves. Mt. Vernon High School and the district's elementary and middle schools are expanding capacity and programming to meet the demands of a rapidly increasing student population. Families moving to McCordsville will find a district that is actively investing in its future — adding facilities and staff to keep pace with one of the fastest-growing residential areas in the state. The district's trajectory is upward in both enrollment and resources, making it a promising choice for families with school-age children.

Commute from McCordsville to Indianapolis

McCordsville residents commute primarily via SR-67 and SR-38, connecting to I-465 and the broader Indianapolis highway network. Downtown Indianapolis is approximately 20 to 25 minutes southwest, while Fishers and the I-69 tech and corporate corridor are accessible in roughly 10 to 15 minutes north — a major convenience for those employed in Fishers or Hamilton County without wanting to pay Hamilton County home prices. The location provides practical access to multiple employment centers across the northeast metro.
