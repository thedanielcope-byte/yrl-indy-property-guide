Shirley sits in eastern Hancock County along State Road 9, just east of Wilkinson and within reasonable driving distance of Indianapolis. With a town population of about 700 and a school district that draws from a broader rural area, Shirley offers genuine small-town living with the kind of country surroundings that have become increasingly rare in the southeastern Indianapolis metro footprint. The town is anchored by its compact downtown, the Eastern Hancock Schools campus that gives the area its own school district identity, and the surrounding working farms and rural roads. For buyers seeking real small-town living, accessible prices, and a school district with its own community identity, Shirley is one of Hancock County's most distinctive small-town options. Living in Shirley, Indiana

Life in Shirley moves at a slower pace than the central Greenfield-McCordsville corridor of Hancock County. The downtown is small but functional, with a handful of locally owned businesses and the kind of friendly small-town atmosphere where neighbors know each other. The surrounding countryside provides quiet rural roads, working farms, and the kind of open agricultural landscape that has become harder to find closer to Indianapolis. The community calendar centers around Friday-night Eastern Hancock Royals athletics, summer festivals, and the steady rhythm of small-town life. For weekend recreation, residents have easy access to the broader Hancock County trail system, and Wilbur Wright State Recreation Area (about 25 minutes east) provides additional outdoor recreation. For buyers who want a quiet small-town setting at attainable prices with eastern Hancock County's rural feel and within reasonable reach of Indianapolis, Shirley offers a distinctive value option.

Shirley Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Shirley real estate market is small and personal, offering strong value within Hancock County. Homes typically range from the $150s for established single-family homes and starter properties to the $250s and beyond for larger homes, updated properties, and rural homes with substantial acreage. The market is small and inventory turns over slowly, with many transactions involving unique properties such as historic homes, working farms, hobby farms, or rural homes with substantial land. For first-time buyers, retirees, remote workers, and anyone willing to trade longer commute time for substantially lower housing costs and real country living, Shirley represents some of Hancock County's strongest value. For investors, the market is thin so resale liquidity is a consideration — but well-located properties continue to attract steady tenant demand from families tied to the school district.

Schools in Shirley, Indiana

Shirley is served by the Eastern Hancock County Community School Corporation, which operates Eastern Hancock Junior-Senior High School and feeder elementary schools. The district is well regarded for its small class sizes, strong community involvement, and competitive athletic programs — the Eastern Hancock Royals consistently field strong programs that draw enthusiastic community support across eastern Hancock County. Families relocating to the area who want a small-school experience often land on Eastern Hancock for exactly that reason. A Your Realty Link agent can help you confirm attendance details for any home you're considering.

Commute from Shirley to Indianapolis

Shirley's commute math is longer than central Hancock County but workable for many buyers. Downtown Indianapolis is approximately 35 miles west — about a 45-minute drive via SR-9 south to I-70 west, or via SR-9 to Greenfield and US-40 west, depending on traffic. Greenfield — Hancock County's seat — sits about 20 minutes west via SR-9. For remote workers, hybrid workers, and anyone willing to accommodate a longer commute in exchange for substantially lower housing costs and real country living, Shirley offers a meaningful trade-off worth considering.
