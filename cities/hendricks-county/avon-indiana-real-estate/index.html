Avon, Indiana has become one of the most sought-after communities on the west side of Indianapolis, and for good reason. Located in eastern Hendricks County along the US-36 corridor, Avon sits approximately 20 miles from downtown Indianapolis — close enough for a practical commute, far enough to feel genuinely suburban. Over the past two decades, Avon has experienced some of the most consistent residential growth in Central Indiana, transforming from a quiet township into a fully developed community with its own identity, amenities, and strong civic pride. Families flock to Avon primarily for its schools, but they stay for the community — the parks, the events, the well-maintained neighborhoods, and the sense that this is a place people choose intentionally. Living in Avon, Indiana

Avon's growth has brought a full complement of amenities to the west side without sacrificing the community feel that drew people here in the first place. Washington Township Park is a popular recreation destination with sports fields, walking paths, and open green space. The US-36 commercial corridor offers a growing selection of restaurants, retail, and services that continue to expand as the residential base matures. Avon is particularly known for its youth sports culture — organized leagues, travel teams, and school athletics all have strong participation. Community events throughout the year bring neighbors together, and the local school system's performing arts program — Avon High School's marching band and drumline have earned national recognition — gives the community a distinctive point of pride that goes beyond typical suburban identity.

Avon Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Avon real estate market reflects strong demand driven largely by school district reputation and community quality. Homes typically range from the $260s for smaller homes in established neighborhoods to the upper $450s for larger new construction homes in the community's growing subdivisions. New construction has been a significant part of Avon's market, with multiple builder communities active in and around the town offering modern floor plans and updated finishes. Resale values in Avon tend to hold well — the school district's consistent appeal keeps demand steady even when the broader market softens. Buyers who purchase in Avon typically find the community a reliable long-term investment.

Schools in Avon, Indiana

Avon is served entirely by Avon Community School Corporation, one of Hendricks County's most respected public school systems. The district spans elementary, middle, and high school levels, with Avon High School standing as its crown jewel. Beyond strong academic programming, Avon High School's performing arts — particularly the nationally competitive marching band and winterguard — have put Avon on the map in ways that extend well beyond Central Indiana. Families who prioritize both academic rigor and extracurricular excellence consistently rate Avon Community School Corporation as one of the main reasons they chose this community over other west-side options.

Commute from Avon to Indianapolis

Avon commuters travel primarily via US-36 east or Ronald Reagan Parkway to reach I-465, placing downtown Indianapolis roughly 20 to 25 minutes away in normal traffic. I-465 connects Avon residents to all points around the metro, and Indianapolis International Airport is easily accessible in approximately 15 minutes — a meaningful convenience for frequent travelers. The west side's highway network makes Avon a practical base for professionals working across the metro.
