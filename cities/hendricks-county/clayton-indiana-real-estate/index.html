📍 S Hendricks County 🏫 Mill Creek Community Schools 🏡 $200s – $350s+ ~40 min to Downtown Indy

Clayton sits in southern Hendricks County along US-40 (the historic National Road), about 35 to 40 minutes west of downtown Indianapolis and roughly 15 minutes south of Plainfield. With a town population of about 1,000 and a school district that draws from a broader rural area, Clayton offers genuine small-town living within reach of the Indianapolis metro. The town is anchored by its compact downtown along Main Street, the surrounding agricultural countryside, and the Mill Creek Community Schools system that brings local students together at the Cascade campus a short drive south. For buyers seeking real rural-fringe living, accessible prices, and a school district with its own identity, Clayton is one of Hendricks County's most affordable small-town options. Living in Clayton, Indiana

Life in Clayton moves at a slower pace than the rapidly growing central Hendricks County corridor. The downtown is small but functional, with locally owned restaurants and shops and the kind of friendly small-town atmosphere where neighbors know each other. The surrounding countryside provides quiet rural roads, working farms, and the kind of open agricultural landscape that has become harder to find closer to the metro. US-40 — the historic National Road that runs through Clayton — connects the town west toward Greencastle and east toward Plainfield and Indianapolis. The annual community calendar includes summer festivals and the steady rhythm of Cascade Cadets athletics. For weekend recreation, residents have easy access to Mill Creek Country Park and the broader Hendricks County trail system. For buyers who want a tranquil small-town setting with Indianapolis access and substantially lower housing costs than central Hendricks County, Clayton offers a distinctive option.

Clayton Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Clayton real estate market is small and personal, offering some of the most affordable options in all of Hendricks County. Homes typically range from the $200s for established single-family homes and starter properties to the $350s and beyond for larger homes, updated properties, and rural homes with substantial acreage. The market is small and inventory turns over slowly, with many transactions involving unique properties such as historic homes near downtown, working farms, hobby farms, or rural homes with land. For first-time buyers, retirees, remote workers, and anyone willing to trade longer commute time for substantially lower housing costs and real country living, Clayton represents some of Hendricks County's strongest value. For investors, the market is thin so resale liquidity is a consideration — but well-located properties continue to attract steady tenant demand.

Schools in Clayton, Indiana

Clayton is served by the Mill Creek Community School Corporation, which operates Cascade High School, Cascade Middle School, and feeder elementary schools throughout the district. The district is well regarded for its small class sizes, strong community involvement, and competitive athletic programs — the Cascade Cadets consistently field strong programs that draw enthusiastic community support across the small towns of southern Hendricks County. Families relocating to southern Hendricks County who want a small-school experience often land on Mill Creek for exactly that reason. A Your Realty Link agent can help you confirm attendance details for any home you're considering.

Commute from Clayton to Indianapolis

Clayton's commute math is longer than central Hendricks County but workable for many buyers. Downtown Indianapolis is approximately 30 miles east — about a 40-minute drive via US-40 east to I-465, depending on traffic. The Plainfield retail and employment corridor sits about 15 minutes east via US-40, putting Hendricks County's major commercial base within reasonable reach. The airport corridor is approximately 25 minutes via SR-39 to I-70. For remote workers, hybrid workers, and anyone willing to accommodate a longer commute in exchange for substantially lower housing costs and real country living, Clayton offers a meaningful trade-off worth considering.
