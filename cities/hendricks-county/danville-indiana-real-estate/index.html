📍 Hendricks County 🏫 Danville Community Schools 🏡 $250s – $400s+ ~30 min to Downtown Indy

Danville is the seat of Hendricks County and one of the most architecturally distinctive small cities in the broader Indianapolis area. Sitting along US-36 about 30 minutes west of downtown Indianapolis, Danville has a population of roughly 10,000 and continues to grow steadily as more buyers discover its combination of historic character, strong schools, and easy commute. The city is anchored by the magnificent Hendricks County Courthouse — a Romanesque limestone landmark completed in 1914 — and a downtown square ringed by restored brick storefronts, locally owned restaurants and shops, and the historic Royal Theater. Danville Community Schools serves the city and surrounding area, and the recently expanded municipal park system provides green space and family recreation. Living in Danville, Indiana

Danville's historic downtown square is one of the strongest small-city downtowns in the Indianapolis metro. The towering Hendricks County Courthouse anchors a square ringed by independent restaurants like the historic Mayberry Cafe and Frank's, plus shops, breweries, and the Royal Theater for live entertainment. Beyond downtown, Ellis Park and the surrounding park system provide pool, sports, and event space, and the Vandalia Trail along the historic National Road provides walking and biking miles. The annual Hendricks County 4-H Fair, the Mayberry Festival, and a steady community calendar keep the city's social rhythm strong. Newer residential growth along US-36 and the SR-39 corridor has brought modern construction at varied price points without overwhelming the city's historic character. For buyers who want a real small-city downtown with full Indianapolis access and strong schools, Danville is consistently one of Hendricks County's strongest options.

Danville Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Danville real estate market offers an unusually wide range of housing styles and price points. Homes typically range from the $250s for established single-family homes and historic homes in older neighborhoods near downtown to the $400s and beyond for newer construction and larger homes in newer subdivisions on the city's edges. Danville's historic neighborhoods near the courthouse square offer real architectural character — restored Victorians, Craftsman bungalows, and early 20th-century homes on tree-lined streets — while the newer subdivisions along US-36 and SR-39 provide modern construction with amenities. Steady demand from buyers seeking Hendricks County living at strong value compared to Avon and Brownsburg has supported consistent appreciation across price points. For investors, Danville's mix of accessible prices and steady tenant demand from families tied to the school district makes the area an active rental market.

Schools in Danville, Indiana

Danville is served by the Danville Community School Corporation, which operates Danville Community High School, Danville Community Middle School, and feeder elementary schools throughout the district. The district is well regarded for its academic, career-technical, and athletic programs, and Danville Community High School fields strong athletic programs — the Warriors — that draw enthusiastic community support. Families relocating to Hendricks County who want a strong public school district at attainable price points often land on Danville Community after their research. Because the district draws from a broader area than just the city limits, a Your Realty Link agent can help you understand which schools serve any home you're considering.

Commute from Danville to Indianapolis

Danville commuters benefit from direct US-36 access, with downtown Indianapolis approximately 20 miles east — about a 30-minute drive under normal conditions. The major north-side employment corridor (Carmel, Fishers) is approximately 35 minutes via I-465, and the airport corridor is roughly 25 minutes via SR-267 south to I-70 east. The Avon and Brownsburg retail and employment corridors sit just 10 to 15 minutes east, putting Hendricks County's major commercial base within easy reach. For buyers prioritizing accessible Indianapolis access with strong schools and small-city character, Danville consistently delivers strong commute math at meaningfully lower price points than Avon or Brownsburg.
