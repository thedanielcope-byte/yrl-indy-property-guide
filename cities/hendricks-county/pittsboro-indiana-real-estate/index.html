📍 N Hendricks County 🏫 Tri-West Hendricks Schools 🏡 $250s – $400s+ ~30 min to Downtown Indy

Pittsboro sits in northern Hendricks County along the I-74 corridor, just north of Brownsburg and within easy reach of both downtown Indianapolis and the Hendricks County employment corridor. With a town population of about 4,000 and steady ongoing growth, Pittsboro offers something appealing in Hendricks County: a small-town atmosphere with strong schools, newer residential growth, and substantially lower housing costs than the central Avon-Brownsburg corridor. The town is anchored by its compact historic downtown along Main Street, the Tri-West Hendricks High School campus (the centerpiece of the North West Hendricks district), and a network of newer subdivisions on the town's edges. Living in Pittsboro, Indiana

Life in Pittsboro reflects the balance of small-town character and Hendricks County convenience. The downtown is small but functional, with locally owned restaurants, shops, and the friendly small-town atmosphere where neighbors know each other. Beyond downtown, Scamahorn Park and the surrounding park system provide green space and recreation, and the town's network of newer subdivisions along the SR-39 corridor has brought modern construction at attainable prices. Tri-West Hendricks Schools — the high school campus sits in town — anchors the community's social calendar with Friday-night Tri-West Bruins football, basketball, and other athletics drawing enthusiastic local support. For weekend recreation, residents have easy access to Hendricks County's broader park and trail system, and the Avon retail and dining corridor sits just 15 minutes south. For buyers who want a small-town atmosphere, strong schools, and direct I-74 commute access without the higher housing costs of Avon and Brownsburg, Pittsboro is consistently one of Hendricks County's strongest value options.

Pittsboro Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Pittsboro real estate market offers strong value for buyers seeking newer construction and Hendricks County schools at attainable prices. Homes typically range from the $250s for established single-family homes and starter properties to the $400s and beyond for newer construction and larger homes in newer subdivisions. The mix of housing styles — historic homes near downtown, mid-century neighborhoods, and newer subdivisions developed in the past 10 to 15 years — gives buyers genuine variety. The combination of accessible prices, Tri-West Hendricks Schools, and ongoing residential development has supported consistent demand and steady appreciation. For investors, Pittsboro offers a workable combination of accessible acquisition prices and steady tenant demand from families tied to the school district. Inventory moves consistently when homes are well-prepared and accurately priced.

Schools in Pittsboro, Indiana

Pittsboro is served by the North West Hendricks School Corporation, which operates Tri-West Hendricks High School, Tri-West Middle School, and feeder elementary schools throughout the district. The district is well regarded for its academic, career-technical, and athletic programs, and Tri-West Bruins athletics consistently field strong programs that draw enthusiastic community support. Families relocating to Hendricks County who want a smaller-district experience with the county's overall reputation often land on Tri-West Hendricks after their research. Because the district draws from a broader area than just Pittsboro town limits, a Your Realty Link agent can help you understand which schools serve any home you're considering.

Commute from Pittsboro to Indianapolis

Pittsboro's commute math is one of its strongest advantages. The town sits along I-74 with direct interstate access at the SR-39 interchange — putting downtown Indianapolis approximately 25 miles east and roughly 30 minutes away under normal conditions. The Avon and Brownsburg retail and employment corridors sit just 15 minutes south, and the airport corridor is approximately 30 minutes via I-465. For buyers prioritizing fast Indianapolis access without the higher housing costs of Avon or Brownsburg, Pittsboro consistently delivers practical commute math at meaningfully lower price points.
