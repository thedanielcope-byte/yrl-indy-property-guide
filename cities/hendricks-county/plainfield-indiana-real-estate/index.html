📍 Hendricks County Seat 🏫 Plainfield Community Schools 🏡 $230s–$400s ~15 min to Downtown Indy

Plainfield, Indiana is the county seat of Hendricks County and one of the most strategically located communities in the entire Indianapolis metro. Positioned directly west of the city along the I-70 corridor, Plainfield has grown into both a major commercial and distribution hub and a welcoming residential community for families, professionals, and commuters. With a population of approximately 35,000, Plainfield is large enough to offer strong amenities while maintaining the accessible pace of a Hendricks County community. The historic US-40 — the old National Road — runs through town, connecting Plainfield's past to its modern identity as one of the region's most active logistics and retail centers. For buyers seeking west-side convenience at competitive prices, Plainfield consistently stands out. Living in Plainfield, Indiana

Plainfield offers a lifestyle defined by accessibility and value. The Indiana Premium Outlets draw shoppers from across the region and anchor a broader retail corridor that gives residents easy access to major national brands without the congestion of downtown shopping. Local parks and recreation facilities serve a growing residential population, and the town's proximity to White Lick Creek and surrounding greenways provides outdoor recreation options close to home. Plainfield's commercial tax base — bolstered by major distribution and logistics employers along the I-70 corridor — helps keep the community financially stable and invested in public services and infrastructure. Residents appreciate that Plainfield offers a full suite of amenities while maintaining housing prices that remain among the most competitive in Hendricks County.

Plainfield Home Prices & Real Estate Market

Plainfield delivers strong value for buyers entering the Hendricks County market. Homes typically range from the $230s for established ranch and traditional two-story homes to the upper $400s for larger, newer construction properties. The market includes a wide variety of home types — from older homes along the historic US-40 corridor to newer subdivisions with modern layouts on the town's developing edges. Buyers who work at or near Indianapolis International Airport find Plainfield particularly appealing, as the roughly 10-minute drive to the airport is among the shortest of any residential community in the metro. The town's strong commercial employment base also creates a steady local demand that supports home values over time.

Schools in Plainfield, Indiana

Plainfield is served by Plainfield Community School Corporation, the public school system that serves the county seat and surrounding township. Plainfield High School anchors the district and supports a range of academic, athletic, and extracurricular programs. The district's elementary and middle school pipeline prepares students well for high school and post-secondary opportunities. Families considering a move to Plainfield will find a district that is community-supported and invested in student outcomes across all grade levels.

Commute from Plainfield to Indianapolis

Plainfield's location directly on I-70 makes it one of the most commuter-efficient communities on the west side. Downtown Indianapolis is approximately 15 to 20 minutes east via I-70, and Indianapolis International Airport is roughly 10 minutes away — a significant advantage for frequent flyers and those employed in aviation or airport-adjacent industries. I-465 connects Plainfield to the full metro ring road, making north, south, and east destinations accessible without navigating surface streets through the city.
