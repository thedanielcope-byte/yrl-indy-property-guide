📍 Johnson County 🏫 Center Grove Schools 🏡 $300s – $600s+ ~25 min to Downtown Indy

Bargersville sits in northern Johnson County along the State Road 135 corridor, just south of Greenwood and within easy reach of downtown Indianapolis — and over the past decade it has emerged as one of the fastest-growing towns in central Indiana. The town's population has grown from roughly 4,500 in 2010 to well over 10,000 today, and continues to add hundreds of new homes each year. Bargersville is anchored by a charming historic downtown along Main Street that has steadily reinvested in itself with locally owned restaurants, breweries, coffee shops, and shops, combined with extensive newer residential growth on the town's edges. The town is served by Center Grove Community Schools — one of Indiana's most consistently top-ranked public school districts — and offers an easy commute to both downtown Indianapolis and the Hamilton County employment corridor. Living in Bargersville, Indiana

Life in Bargersville reflects the experience of being part of one of the most exciting growth stories in central Indiana. The historic downtown along Main Street — with restored brick storefronts housing places like Taxman Brewing, the Mill Town Tavern, and a growing list of independent restaurants — gives the town a real social center and a steady community calendar of events. The Bargersville Town Hall and the nearby Johnson County Park provide civic and recreational anchors. Beyond downtown, the town has grown thoughtfully — newer subdivisions include extensive trail networks, community pools, and parks, and large-scale projects along SR-135 and CR-144 have brought modern construction at varied price points. The State Road 135 commercial corridor provides everyday shopping, dining, and services that just keep expanding. For buyers who want a real downtown with character, strong schools, newer construction, and a fast commute to Indianapolis, Bargersville is consistently one of the strongest options in the south metro.

Bargersville Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Bargersville real estate market reflects the town's high-demand, growth-driven character. Homes typically range from the $300s for newer construction starter homes, townhomes, and patio homes in newer subdivisions to the $600s and beyond for larger homes on premium lots in premier communities. The mid-range — well-appointed family homes in established sections of newer subdivisions — generally falls in the $400s to $500s. Historic homes in the older parts of town near downtown also command real demand thanks to walkability and character. The combination of new construction supply, strong demand from buyers seeking Center Grove schools at lower price points than other Center Grove communities, and continued ongoing infrastructure investment has supported consistent appreciation. For investors, newer Bargersville homes also draw a steady rental tenant pool. Inventory moves consistently when homes are well-prepared and accurately priced.

Schools in Bargersville, Indiana

Most of Bargersville is served by the Center Grove Community School Corporation — one of Indiana's most consistently top-ranked public school districts. Center Grove operates Center Grove High School, Center Grove Middle School North and Center, and a network of feeder elementary schools throughout its broad attendance area. The district is well regarded for its academic, athletic, and arts programs, and Center Grove High School fields strong athletic programs that draw enthusiastic community support. Families relocating to Johnson County who prioritize schools consistently land within the Center Grove district after their research. Because the district draws from a wide area that extends well beyond town limits and some portions of Bargersville may fall within other districts, a Your Realty Link agent can help you understand which schools serve any home you're considering and recommend confirming current attendance details directly with the district.

Commute from Bargersville to Indianapolis

Bargersville commuters benefit from a strong location. State Road 135 runs north into Greenwood and Indianapolis, with downtown Indianapolis approximately 20 miles north — about a 25 to 30-minute drive under normal conditions. SR-37 (now upgraded to interstate-grade I-69) is accessible just east of the town, providing fast access to the broader metro. I-65 sits a short drive east via county roads, putting the major south-side and downtown employment corridors within easy reach. For buyers prioritizing accessible Indianapolis access with strong schools and quality-of-life amenities, Bargersville consistently delivers strong commute math — and the ongoing I-69 corridor improvements continue to shorten the practical drive time.
