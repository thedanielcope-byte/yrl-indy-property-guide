📍 SE Johnson County 🛍 Edinburgh Premium Outlets 🏡 $180s – $300s+ ~40 min to Downtown Indy

Edinburgh sits at the southeastern edge of Johnson County along the I-65 corridor, straddling the Johnson/Shelby/Bartholomew County boundaries roughly 35 to 40 minutes south of downtown Indianapolis. With a town population of about 5,000, Edinburgh is best known regionally as the home of the Edinburgh Premium Outlets — a major shopping destination that draws visitors from across central and southern Indiana — and as a practical, affordable bedroom community for the I-65 corridor between Indianapolis and Columbus. The town is anchored by its compact historic downtown, the Edinburgh Community Schools campus, and a residential mix that runs from established mid-century neighborhoods to country properties along the surrounding rural roads. Camp Atterbury sits just west of town and provides employment for many area residents. Living in Edinburgh, Indiana

Daily life in Edinburgh is shaped by a practical combination of small-town character and major-corridor convenience. The historic downtown is small but functional, with locally owned restaurants, shops, and the kind of friendly Main Street where neighbors know each other. The Edinburgh Premium Outlets sit just off I-65 and bring substantial visitor traffic, employment, and easy access to dining and retail. Camp Atterbury — the Indiana National Guard training facility — sits a short drive west of town and provides employment for area residents. The annual community calendar includes summer festivals along Main Street, Friday-night Edinburgh Lancers football, and the steady rhythm of small-town life. The Johnson County Park system and the recreation opportunities along the Big Blue River add accessible outdoor recreation. For buyers prioritizing affordability, fast I-65 access in both directions, and a real small-town environment, Edinburgh is consistently one of the more practical options at the south end of Johnson County.

Edinburgh Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Edinburgh real estate market is one of the more affordable in Johnson County, particularly relative to the rapidly appreciating central corridor through Greenwood and Bargersville. Homes typically range from the $180s for established single-family homes and starter properties to the $300s and beyond for larger or updated homes and country properties with acreage. The town's mix of housing — mid-century brick ranches, two-story homes from the 1960s and 1970s, historic homes near downtown, and pockets of newer construction — gives buyers genuine variety at prices that have become hard to find closer to the metro core. For investors, Edinburgh has historically offered a workable combination of accessible acquisition prices, steady rental demand from families tied to the local school district, and a small but steady visitor and worker pool tied to the outlets and Camp Atterbury. The market moves consistently when homes are well-prepared and accurately priced.

Schools in Edinburgh, Indiana

Edinburgh is served by the Edinburgh Community School Corporation, which operates Edinburgh Community High School, Edinburgh Community Middle School, and Edinburgh Community Elementary — all on a compact campus serving the town. The district is well regarded for its small class sizes, strong community involvement, and competitive athletic programs. Families relocating to southern Johnson County who want a small-school experience often land on Edinburgh for exactly that reason. Because the district draws from a broader area than just the town and some surrounding addresses may fall within other Johnson, Shelby, or Bartholomew County districts, a Your Realty Link agent can help you confirm attendance details for any home you're considering.

Commute from Edinburgh to Indianapolis & Columbus

Edinburgh's commute math benefits from its position along the I-65 corridor between Indianapolis and Columbus. Downtown Indianapolis is roughly 30 miles north — about a 40-minute drive via I-65 under normal conditions. Columbus, Indiana — home to Cummins Inc. and a significant employment base of its own — is approximately 15 miles south, about a 20-minute drive via I-65, giving Edinburgh residents the unusual flexibility of two genuine job markets within reasonable commuting distance. The major south-side Indianapolis employment corridors and downtown are directly accessible, and the Greenwood retail corridor sits about 20 minutes north. For buyers prioritizing flexible commute options, fast interstate access, and substantially lower housing costs than central Johnson County, Edinburgh delivers a practical package.
