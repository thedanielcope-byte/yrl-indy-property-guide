📍 SW Johnson County 🏫 Indian Creek Schools 🏡 $200s – $350s+ ~40 min to Downtown Indy

Trafalgar sits in southwestern Johnson County along State Road 135, tucked between Bargersville to the north and Brown County to the south. With a town population of about 1,000 and a school district that draws from a broader rural area, Trafalgar offers genuine small-town living with the kind of country surroundings that have become increasingly rare in the Indianapolis metro footprint. The town is anchored by its compact downtown along Main Street, the Indian Creek Schools campus that serves families across the southern part of Johnson County, and the surrounding hills and woods that begin the transition into Brown County's famed terrain. Camp Atterbury sits a short drive south, and the Hoosier National Forest and Brown County State Park are within easy weekend reach. Living in Trafalgar, Indiana

Life in Trafalgar moves at a different pace than the rapidly growing northern parts of Johnson County. The downtown is small but functional, with a handful of locally owned restaurants and shops along Main Street and the kind of friendly small-town atmosphere where neighbors actually know each other. The surrounding countryside provides quiet rural roads, working farms, and the rolling, wooded terrain that connects to Brown County's distinctive landscape just to the south. Camp Atterbury — the Indiana National Guard training facility — sits a short drive south of town and provides employment for many area residents. For weekend recreation, Brown County State Park, Yellowwood State Forest, and the Hoosier National Forest are within easy reach. The annual community calendar includes summer festivals and the steady rhythm of Indian Creek Braves athletics. For buyers who want a quiet small-town setting with access to the Indianapolis metro and immediate access to some of Indiana's best outdoor recreation, Trafalgar is one of Johnson County's most distinctive options.

Trafalgar Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Trafalgar real estate market is small, personal, and well-priced for buyers seeking real value within reach of the Indianapolis metro. Homes typically range from the $200s for established single-family homes and starter properties within town limits to the $350s and beyond for larger homes, newer construction, and rural properties with substantial acreage. The mix of housing styles — historic homes near downtown, mid-century neighborhoods, newer construction on the town's edges, and country properties on rolling lots — gives buyers genuine variety. Town homes near the school campus move when they come up, while rural properties with acreage attract buyers seeking space, gardens, hobby farms, and the beginning-of-Brown-County terrain. For investors, the market is thinner than larger nearby cities, so resale liquidity is a consideration — but well-located properties continue to attract steady tenant demand from families tied to the school district.

Schools in Trafalgar, Indiana

Trafalgar is served by the Nineveh-Hensley-Jackson United School Corporation, which operates Indian Creek Senior High School, Indian Creek Middle School, and feeder elementary schools throughout the district. The district is well regarded for its small class sizes, strong community involvement, and competitive athletic programs. Indian Creek's athletic teams — the Braves — consistently field strong programs that draw enthusiastic community support. Families relocating to southern Johnson County who want a small-school experience often land on Indian Creek for exactly that reason. A Your Realty Link agent can help you confirm attendance details for any home you're considering.

Commute from Trafalgar to Indianapolis

Trafalgar's commute math is longer than the central Johnson County corridor but workable for many buyers. Downtown Indianapolis is approximately 30 miles north — about a 40-minute drive via SR-135 north to I-65 or via Bluff Road, depending on traffic. Greenwood and the major south-side Johnson County employment corridor are approximately 20 minutes north via SR-135. Bargersville, with its expanding dining and retail scene, is about 15 minutes north. For remote workers, hybrid workers, and anyone whose work can accommodate a slightly longer commute in exchange for genuine country living and substantially lower housing costs than central Johnson County, Trafalgar offers a real trade-off worth considering.
