Whiteland Indiana Real Estate & Homes for Sale

📍 Johnson County 🏫 Clark-Pleasant Schools 🏡 $250s – $400s+ ~25 min to Downtown Indy

Whiteland sits in northern Johnson County along the I-65 corridor, just east of Greenwood and roughly 25 minutes south of downtown Indianapolis. With a town population of about 5,000 and steady ongoing growth, Whiteland has emerged as one of the most practical buyer options in Johnson County — offering newer construction, an investing-and-growing school district, easy interstate access, and prices meaningfully lower than nearby Greenwood and Center Grove communities. The town is anchored by Whiteland Community High School and the Clark-Pleasant school district campus, the US-31/SR-44 commercial corridor, and a network of newer subdivisions that have brought modern construction at varied price points. Living in Whiteland, Indiana

Life in Whiteland is shaped by the practical balance of suburban convenience and continued residential growth. The town offers a quiet residential feel with established neighborhoods and an expanding network of newer subdivisions on its edges, plus access to the major retail and dining corridors of Greenwood just a few minutes north. The Clark-Pleasant Community Park system provides green space and family recreation throughout the district, and the town's central location puts everyday shopping, dining, and services within minutes via US-31 and SR-44. Whiteland Community High School athletics give the town a strong community focal point — Friday-night football, basketball, and other sports draw enthusiastic local support. For buyers who want newer construction, a growing school district, easy I-65 access, and prices below the Center Grove and Greenwood markets, Whiteland is consistently one of Johnson County's most practical options.

Whiteland Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Whiteland real estate market offers strong value for buyers seeking newer construction and accessible commuting at attainable prices. Homes typically range from the $250s for established single-family homes and starter properties to the $400s and beyond for newer construction and larger homes in newer subdivisions. The mid-range — well-appointed family homes in newer subdivisions developed in the past 10 to 15 years — generally falls in the $300s to $400s. The combination of new construction supply, demand from buyers seeking Johnson County living at lower price points than nearby Greenwood and Bargersville, and proximity to the I-65 corridor employment base has supported consistent demand and steady appreciation. For investors, Whiteland's accessible prices and steady rental demand from families tied to the school district make the area an active rental market.

Schools in Whiteland, Indiana

Whiteland is served by the Clark-Pleasant Community Schools, which operates Whiteland Community High School, Clark-Pleasant Middle School, and feeder elementary schools throughout the district. The district has invested significantly in academic, career-technical, and athletic programs in recent years, including a new high school facility and ongoing expansion. Whiteland Community High School fields strong athletic programs that draw enthusiastic community support. Families relocating to Johnson County who want a growing, well-supported district at price points below the Center Grove premium often land on Clark-Pleasant after their research. Because the district draws from a broader area than just the town, a Your Realty Link agent can help you understand which schools serve any home you're considering.

Commute from Whiteland to Indianapolis

Whiteland's commute math is one of its strongest advantages. The town sits along US-31 with I-65 access via SR-44 just a few minutes east — putting downtown Indianapolis approximately 20 miles north and roughly 25 minutes away under normal conditions. The major south-side and downtown employment corridors along I-65 are directly accessible, and the major Greenwood retail and employment corridor sits just minutes north via US-31. For buyers prioritizing fast Indianapolis access without the higher housing costs of Greenwood or Center Grove communities, Whiteland consistently delivers practical commute math at meaningfully lower price points.
