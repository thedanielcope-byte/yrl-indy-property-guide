Alexandria sits in northern Madison County along State Road 9, about 12 miles north of Anderson and roughly 50 minutes northeast of downtown Indianapolis. With a population of about 5,000, Alexandria is the largest city in northern Madison County and one of the more affordable markets in all of central Indiana. The city has a historic industrial heritage — once known as the "Small Town with the Big Heart" — and continues to function as a working small city with locally owned businesses, a compact historic downtown along Harrison Street, and the campus of Alexandria-Monroe Junior-Senior High School serving families across the surrounding area. Surrounding farmland and small lakes give the area a quietly rural feel just outside the city limits. Living in Alexandria, Indiana

Life in Alexandria reflects the working small-city character that defines northern Madison County. The historic downtown along Harrison Street includes locally owned restaurants, shops, the historic Alexandria-Monroe Public Library, and the kind of friendly small-city atmosphere where neighbors know each other. Beyond downtown, Alexandria offers an unusually broad mix of residential neighborhoods — historic homes in older parts of the city, mid-century neighborhoods, and pockets of newer construction on the city's outskirts. The Beulah Park and the city's network of smaller parks provide green space and recreation, and Killbuck Creek runs through town offering fishing and casual recreation. The annual community calendar includes summer festivals, the Alexandria-Monroe Tigers Friday-night football, and a steady community rhythm. For buyers prioritizing affordability, real small-city character, and a school district with a strong community identity, Alexandria delivers one of the most accessible packages in central Indiana.

Alexandria Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Alexandria real estate market is one of the most affordable inside reasonable driving distance of the Indianapolis metro. Homes typically range from the $130s for established single-family homes, starter properties, and historic homes in older parts of the city to the $280s and beyond for larger or updated homes and rural properties with substantial acreage. The mix of housing styles — historic homes near downtown, mid-century brick ranches and two-stories from the 1960s and 1970s, and country properties on the outskirts — gives buyers genuine variety at price points that have become hard to find anywhere closer to Indianapolis. For first-time buyers, retirees on fixed incomes, value-focused families, and investors evaluating accessible rental properties, Alexandria delivers strong value. For investors, the combination of accessible acquisition prices and steady tenant demand from families tied to the local school district makes Alexandria an active rental market.

Schools in Alexandria, Indiana

Alexandria is served by the Alexandria Community School Corporation, which operates Alexandria-Monroe Junior-Senior High School and feeder elementary schools throughout the district. The district is well regarded for its small class sizes, strong community involvement, and competitive athletic programs — the Alexandria-Monroe Tigers consistently field strong programs that draw enthusiastic community support across northern Madison County. Families relocating to the area who want a small-school experience often land on Alexandria for exactly that reason. Commute from Alexandria to Indianapolis

Alexandria's commute math is longer than central Madison County but workable for many buyers. Downtown Indianapolis is approximately 40 miles south — about a 50-minute drive via SR-9 south to Anderson, then I-69 south to the metro, depending on traffic. The Anderson employment corridor (including Anderson University, Hoosier Park, and Community Hospital Anderson) is approximately 20 minutes south via SR-9. Elwood — Madison County's other northern small city — is about 10 minutes west via SR-28. For remote workers, hybrid workers, retirees, and anyone willing to accommodate a longer commute in exchange for substantially lower housing costs and genuine small-city character, Alexandria offers a meaningful trade-off worth considering.
