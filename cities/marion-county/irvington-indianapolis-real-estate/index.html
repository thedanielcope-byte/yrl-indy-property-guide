📍 Marion County 🏛 Historic District 🏡 $150s – $350s+ ~10 min to Downtown Indy

Irvington is one of Indianapolis's most distinctive and storied neighborhoods, tucked along the historic National Road (East Washington Street) on the city's east side, just five miles from downtown. Founded in 1870 and named for author Washington Irving, Irvington was designed with curving, tree-lined streets — a deliberate departure from the city's standard grid — and that romantic, village-like layout still defines the neighborhood today. It's a place where Queen Anne Victorians, Craftsman bungalows, and Tudor cottages sit side by side, and where neighbors gather for the famous Irvington Halloween Festival each fall. Living in Irvington, Indianapolis

Life in Irvington revolves around its walkable, independent business district along Washington Street, where locally owned coffee shops, bookstores, breweries, and restaurants give the neighborhood a genuine small-town feel inside the big city. The Irvington Historic District — one of the largest in Indiana — anchors a strong sense of identity and pride. Residents enjoy Ellenberger Park with its pool, sports courts, and mature shade trees, plus the active Irvington Development Organization that keeps community events humming year-round. The annual Halloween Festival draws tens of thousands of visitors, and the monthly farmers market and art fairs reinforce the creative, close-knit culture. For buyers who want character, community, and a short commute downtown, Irvington is hard to beat.

Irvington Home Prices & Real Estate Market

Irvington offers some of the most characterful housing stock in Indianapolis at attainable prices. Homes typically range from the $150s for charming bungalows and projects in need of restoration to the $350s and higher for fully renovated historic homes on generous, mature lots. The neighborhood is a magnet for buyers who appreciate original woodwork, leaded glass, built-ins, and the craftsmanship of early-1900s construction. Because Irvington sits within a designated historic district, many homes retain their architectural integrity — a quality increasingly rare and valued in today's market. The area has seen steady reinvestment over the past two decades, with restoration-minded buyers and first-time homeowners both drawn to its blend of affordability and authenticity.

Schools in Irvington, Indianapolis

Irvington is served primarily by Indianapolis Public Schools (IPS), with a number of charter, magnet, and private school options available to families throughout the east side. The neighborhood has a long educational legacy — it was once home to Butler University before the campus relocated. Today, families in Irvington often weigh a range of school choices, and a Your Realty Link agent can help you understand the options near any home you're considering. Because school assignment and choice programs can change, we always recommend confirming current enrollment details directly with the district when a specific school matters to your decision.

Commute from Irvington to Downtown Indianapolis

One of Irvington's biggest advantages is its proximity to the urban core. Downtown Indianapolis is roughly five miles away — about a 10-to-15-minute drive via East Washington Street (US-40) or nearby I-70, which runs just south of the neighborhood. That easy access makes Irvington especially appealing to downtown professionals, hospital and university staff, and anyone who wants city convenience without a long commute. The neighborhood is also served by IndyGo bus routes along the Washington Street corridor.
