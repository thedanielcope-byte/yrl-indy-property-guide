Lawrence Indiana Real Estate & Homes for Sale

Buying or selling in Lawrence? Your Realty Link's our agents know northeast Indianapolis — from the Fort Ben heritage neighborhoods to newer development along the Hamilton County line.

📍 Marion County 🏫 MSD Lawrence Township 🏡 $160s–$320s Fort Harrison State Park

Lawrence, Indiana is a city of roughly 50,000 people occupying the northeast corner of Marion County, sharing its northern boundary with Hamilton County and sitting approximately 15 miles from downtown Indianapolis. Lawrence is defined in large part by its most significant landmark: Fort Benjamin Harrison, the former U.S. Army post whose redevelopment has given the city a distinctive character unlike any other Marion County community. The former base now encompasses residential neighborhoods with historic officer-era homes, the beautiful Fort Harrison State Park, and the Lawrence government complex. Beyond the Fort Ben area, Lawrence is a diverse, established community with a broad mix of housing types, a strong school district with two high school campuses, and convenient access to both Indianapolis and the growing employment centers in Fishers and Noblesville to the north. Living in Lawrence, Indiana

Fort Harrison State Park is the crown jewel of Lawrence's lifestyle offering — a beautifully wooded 1,700-acre park with hiking and mountain biking trails, a championship golf course, and the historic Garrison Chapel, all within the city limits. This is an unusual amenity for a city of Lawrence's density, and it gives residents a genuine outdoor escape minutes from home. The Geist area to the north provides additional recreation, and Lawrence's location on the I-69 corridor makes Fishers and Noblesville dining and entertainment easily accessible. The community itself is diverse and well-established, with active neighborhood associations, community events along Pendleton Pike, and a school district that is deeply invested in serving a broad range of students. Lawrence rewards buyers who look past the surface and appreciate genuine community assets over marketing polish.

Lawrence Home Prices & Real Estate Market

Lawrence offers some of the most compelling value in northeast Marion County. Homes typically range from the $160s for smaller, older homes in established neighborhoods to the upper $320s for larger updated properties and homes in the Fort Ben heritage area. The historic homes near the former Fort Benjamin Harrison officer quarters are particularly notable — well-built, architecturally distinctive, and often featuring larger lots than typical suburban builds of the same era. The broader Lawrence market is diverse, with ranch homes, split-levels, and more recent construction all represented across the city's neighborhoods. Investors and first-time buyers consistently find Lawrence's price-to-location ratio among the best in the northeast Indianapolis market.

Schools in Lawrence, Indiana

Lawrence is served by MSD of Lawrence Township, one of Marion County's stronger urban school districts. The district operates two high schools — Lawrence Central High School and Lawrence North High School — both of which have strong academic and athletic reputations. Lawrence North in particular has produced notable alumni in athletics and has a tradition of excellence in basketball. The district's elementary and middle school campuses serve a diverse student population with programming designed to prepare students for college and career. For families considering northeast Indianapolis, Lawrence Township's two-high-school structure means meaningful options within the same district.

Commute from Lawrence to Indianapolis

Lawrence commuters have multiple highway options. I-465 borders the city on its west and south sides, providing quick access to the metro ring road and downtown Indianapolis in approximately 15 to 20 minutes. I-70 and I-69 are both accessible, opening up east-side and northeast corridor employment centers including Fishers and Noblesville with ease. For professionals who work anywhere from downtown to the Hamilton County technology corridor, Lawrence's position at the center of the northeast metro makes it a practical and efficient home base.
