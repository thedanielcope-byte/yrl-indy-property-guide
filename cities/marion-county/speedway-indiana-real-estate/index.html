Speedway Indiana Real Estate & Homes for Sale

There is no community quite like Speedway, Indiana. Your Realty Link's our agents help buyers and sellers navigate this one-of-a-kind Marion County town with local expertise and full MLS access.

📍 Marion County 🏫 Speedway Schools 🏡 $150s–$280s Home of the Indianapolis 500

Speedway, Indiana is unlike any other town in America. Completely surrounded by the northwest side of Indianapolis, this incorporated town of roughly 12,000 people is defined — in the most literal sense — by the Indianapolis Motor Speedway, the legendary 2.5-mile oval that hosts the Indianapolis 500 each Memorial Day weekend. The IMS campus occupies an enormous footprint at the center of the community, and its presence shapes everything about Speedway's identity, economy, and culture. Yet for the roughly 50 weeks of the year when race events are not taking place, Speedway is a quiet, affordable, tight-knit residential community with established neighborhoods, a strong sense of local pride, and convenient access to downtown Indianapolis just 10 miles to the east. Living in Speedway, Indiana

Living in Speedway means embracing an identity that is tied to motorsports in a way no other residential community can claim. The IMS Hall of Fame Museum is open year-round, and the speedway's iconic pagoda and grandstands are a permanent backdrop to daily life in the town. During the month of May — Indianapolis 500 month — the town comes alive with visitors, events, and the unmistakable energy of one of the world's most famous sporting events. Many Speedway residents have developed traditions around race week that go back generations. Outside of race season, the community is defined by its neighborhoods: well-maintained homes on tree-lined streets, longtime residents who know their neighbors, and a town government that operates with genuine responsiveness to its community. The proximity to Eagle Creek Park to the north adds meaningful outdoor recreation access.

Speedway Home Prices & Real Estate Market

Speedway offers genuine affordability with a location that is difficult to beat for northwest-side Indianapolis access. Homes typically range from the $150s for smaller, well-kept bungalows and ranch homes in the town's established neighborhoods to the upper $280s for larger or more recently updated properties. The housing stock reflects the community's mid-century development history — solidly built homes with character that often have larger lot sizes than comparable-priced new construction elsewhere in the metro. The investor and rental market in Speedway is active, supported in part by demand from racing industry professionals, IMS employees, and visitors who rent homes during race events. For buyers seeking affordability with a northwest-side location, Speedway consistently delivers strong value per square foot.

Schools in Speedway, Indiana

Like Beech Grove, Speedway maintains its own independent school system — Speedway Schools — operating entirely separately from Indianapolis Public Schools. Speedway High School is the anchor of the district and carries the community's identity in the same way the IMS defines the town itself: with a distinctive local character that residents take pride in. The district serves elementary through high school students within the town's boundaries. For buyers who want Marion County affordability with the benefit of an independent community school district, Speedway Schools is a meaningful part of the value proposition.

Commute from Speedway to Indianapolis

Speedway's location immediately west of I-465 and the northwest side of Indianapolis puts downtown approximately 10 to 15 minutes east via I-465 or West 38th Street. The Ben Davis corridor and US-136 provide surface street access to multiple west-side employment centers, and I-465 connects Speedway to every quadrant of the metro efficiently. For professionals working anywhere from downtown to the northwest tech and corporate campus corridor, Speedway's highway access is excellent relative to its home prices.
