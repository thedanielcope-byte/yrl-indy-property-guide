Cloverdale sits in southern Putnam County right at the I-70 corridor, just south of Greencastle and within easy reach of Indianapolis, Terre Haute, and Bloomington. With a town population of about 2,000 and a school district that draws from a broader rural area, Cloverdale offers something practical: a small-town atmosphere with direct interstate access, affordable housing, and proximity to some of Indiana's best outdoor recreation. The town is anchored by its compact downtown, the Cloverdale Community Schools campus, and the surrounding rural countryside. Nearby Lieber State Recreation Area on Cagles Mill Lake and Cataract Falls — Indiana's largest waterfall — provide outstanding outdoor recreation just minutes from town. Living in Cloverdale, Indiana

Life in Cloverdale reflects the balance of small-town character and direct interstate convenience. The downtown is small but functional, with locally owned restaurants and shops along Main Street and the kind of friendly small-town atmosphere where neighbors know each other. Beyond downtown, the town offers established residential streets and surrounding country properties on rolling rural lots. The Cloverdale community calendar centers around Friday-night Cloverdale Clovers athletics, summer festivals, and the steady rhythm of small-town life. For weekend recreation, residents have exceptional access to outdoor amenities — Cataract Falls (Indiana's largest waterfall) sits just 10 minutes north, Lieber State Recreation Area on Cagles Mill Lake provides boating, fishing, and camping, and the broader Putnam County trail system continues to expand. For buyers prioritizing affordability, direct I-70 access, real small-town character, and access to outstanding outdoor recreation, Cloverdale delivers a distinctive package.

Cloverdale Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Cloverdale real estate market is small and personal, offering strong value within Putnam County. Homes typically range from the $150s for established single-family homes and starter properties to the $280s and beyond for larger homes, updated properties, and rural homes with substantial acreage. The mix of housing styles — historic homes near downtown, mid-century brick ranches, newer infill construction, and country properties on the outskirts — gives buyers variety at prices that have become hard to find anywhere closer to Indianapolis. For first-time buyers, retirees, remote workers, and anyone willing to accommodate the longer commute in exchange for substantially lower housing costs and country living, Cloverdale delivers strong value. For investors, the market is small but well-located properties continue to attract steady tenant demand from families tied to the school district and local economy.

Schools in Cloverdale, Indiana

Cloverdale is served by the Cloverdale Community School Corporation, which operates Cloverdale High School, Cloverdale Middle School, and feeder elementary schools. The district is well regarded for its small class sizes, strong community involvement, and competitive athletic programs — the Cloverdale Clovers consistently field strong programs that draw enthusiastic community support across southern Putnam County. Families relocating to southern Putnam County who want a small-school experience often land on Cloverdale Community Schools for exactly that reason. A Your Realty Link agent can help you confirm attendance details for any home you're considering.

Commute from Cloverdale to Indianapolis

Cloverdale's commute math is one of the strongest in rural Putnam County thanks to direct I-70 access. Downtown Indianapolis is approximately 45 miles east — about a 50-minute drive via I-70 east, depending on traffic. Greencastle sits about 15 minutes north. Terre Haute is approximately 40 minutes west via I-70. Bloomington is about 45 minutes south via US-231. The dual-direction access via I-70 gives Cloverdale residents flexibility that's unusual for a small town. For remote workers, hybrid workers, and anyone whose work can accommodate the longer drive in exchange for substantially lower housing costs and country character, Cloverdale offers a meaningful trade-off.
