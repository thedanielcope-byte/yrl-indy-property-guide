Holliday Farms is among the most prominent luxury communities in the Indianapolis metro — a modern, high-end development in Zionsville built around a Pete Dye-designed golf course. With upscale custom homes, resort-caliber amenities, and Zionsville's top-tier schools, Holliday Farms has become a marquee address in Boone County. Your Realty Link — an Indianapolis-based MIBOR brokerage led by Daniel Cope — helps buyers and sellers navigate this distinctive luxury market.

Living in Holliday Farms

Holliday Farms delivers a resort-style lifestyle in one of the metro's most prestigious towns. The community is organized around its Pete Dye golf course and a slate of high-end amenities, with modern luxury homes set on generous lots and designed to a level of finish that stands out even in affluent Zionsville. Residents enjoy the exclusivity and amenity depth of a private-club community while staying minutes from Zionsville's charming brick Main Street, boutiques, and restaurants. It appeals to executive buyers, golfers, and anyone seeking a premier new-luxury address in Boone County.

Holliday Farms Homes &amp; Real Estate Market

Holliday Farms sits firmly in the luxury tier. Homes generally start in the $700s and range well into the seven figures, with the largest custom estates reaching $2 million and above. The community's newer construction, architectural quality, and golf-course setting support premium pricing, and demand for luxury inventory in Zionsville remains strong. Because each home is distinctive and the luxury market moves differently than the broader market, working with an agent experienced in high-end transactions is especially valuable here — for pricing a sale accurately or evaluating a purchase.

Schools &amp; Location

Holliday Farms is served by Zionsville Community Schools, one of the most sought-after districts in Indiana and a key draw for families at every price point. Attendance boundaries can change, so we recommend confirming the assigned schools for a specific address. The community's location gives residents quick access to Zionsville's village, the US-421/Michigan Road corridor, and I-65, with downtown Indianapolis generally around 30 minutes away.

Amenities &amp; Lifestyle

The centerpiece of Holliday Farms is its Pete Dye golf course — a nationally recognized designer whose courses are prized by serious golfers — complemented by upscale community amenities and social spaces. The overall experience is designed to feel like a private resort, with the recreation, dining, and gathering places that define club living. For buyers who want new-construction luxury, championship golf, and Zionsville schools in one package, Holliday Farms is one of the metro's premier options.
