Avon vs Brownsburg: Which Hendricks County Town?

The west side's two most-compared communities — nearly identical on price and schools, genuinely different on commute and character.

📍 Hendricks County 🏡 $250s – $450s 🏫 Two Strong Districts

Quick Answer

Avon and Brownsburg are remarkably close on price ($250s–$450s) and both have well-regarded school districts, so the real deciders are your commute corridor and the feel you want. Choose Avon for the US-36 retail corridor and a quick I-465 hop. Choose Brownsburg for I-74 access, a walkable Main Street downtown, and slightly lower entry prices.

If you're buying on the west side, you're almost certainly comparing Avon and Brownsburg. They sit minutes apart in Hendricks County, occupy nearly the same price band, and both are known primarily for their schools. Buyers frequently tour both on the same day and struggle to articulate the difference.

There is one, and it's mostly about which highway you'll drive every morning.

Commute: The Actual Deciding Factor

Avon runs along US-36 with quick access to I-465, which suits commuters heading to the west side, the airport area, and downtown via the 465 loop. Brownsburg sits on I-74, also feeding I-465, which is the better setup if you're heading northwest or need the interstate rather than a commercial arterial.

US-36 through Avon is a busy retail corridor — convenient for errands, slower at peak hours. I-74 from Brownsburg is a genuine interstate run. For many buyers this single difference settles it, and it's worth driving both at your actual commute time before deciding.

Home Prices: Nearly Identical

Avon typically runs from the $260s to the upper $450s; Brownsburg from the $250s to the upper $430s. Brownsburg's entry point is marginally lower, but the overlap is so substantial that at most budgets you'll find comparable homes in both.

Avon's demand is driven heavily by school district reputation, with newer construction toward the upper end of its range. Brownsburg offers a solid spread from established neighborhoods to newer builds on more generous lots. Compare listings on our Avon and Brownsburg pages.

Character: Retail Corridor vs Main Street

Avon's growth has brought a full complement of amenities without losing its community feel. Washington Township Park anchors recreation with sports fields and walking paths, and the US-36 corridor supplies restaurants and retail. It is convenient and family-oriented, but its center of gravity is commercial.

Brownsburg has a more traditional town center. The downtown along Main Street and Jefferson Street has grown into a destination for locally owned restaurants and shops, giving Brownsburg something closer to a walkable core. The town is also home to Lucas Oil Raceway, and there's a genuine motorsports heritage here that shows up in the local identity.

Schools

Avon Community Schools and Brownsburg Community Schools are both strong and are the primary reason most families choose either town. Neither is a weak choice. If schools are your driver, tour both and talk to families in each rather than deciding from a ranking table — the districts are close enough that fit matters more than any published gap.

Choose Avon If…

  • You commute west or use I-465 heading south
  • You want retail, dining, and services within a few minutes
  • Newer construction at the upper end of the range appeals
  • Washington Township Park fits how your family spends weekends

Choose Brownsburg If…

  • You commute on I-74 or head northwest
  • A walkable Main Street downtown matters to you
  • You want a marginally lower entry price
  • You like the town's community-and-racing identity

The Honest Verdict

These two are genuinely close, and you would not be making a mistake with either. Drive both commutes at rush hour, walk Brownsburg's Main Street, and drive the US-36 corridor in Avon. Whichever daily routine you'd rather live decides it — the housing and the schools won't.

Frequently Asked Questions — Avon vs Brownsburg

Is Avon or Brownsburg more expensive?

They are very close. Avon typically runs from the $260s to the upper $450s and Brownsburg from the $250s to the upper $430s. Brownsburg's entry point is slightly lower, but the ranges overlap so much that most buyers will find comparable homes in both.

Which has better schools, Avon or Brownsburg?

Both Avon Community Schools and Brownsburg Community Schools are well-regarded and are the main reason families choose either town. Neither is a weak choice, and the districts are close enough that visiting both and talking to local families is more useful than comparing rankings.

Which is a better commute to Indianapolis?

It depends on your destination. Avon sits on US-36 with quick I-465 access, which suits the west side and airport area. Brownsburg sits on I-74, which is better if you head northwest or prefer an interstate run over a commercial arterial. Driving both at your real commute time is the best test.

What is Brownsburg known for?

Brownsburg is known for its schools, its walkable Main Street downtown with locally owned restaurants and shops, and Lucas Oil Raceway, which gives the town a genuine motorsports heritage.
