Greenwood vs Franklin: Convenience or Value?

Johnson County's two anchors compared — a major south-side retail hub versus a historic county seat where your budget goes considerably further.

📍 Johnson County 🏡 $180s – $450s 🎓 College Town vs Retail Hub

Quick Answer

Choose Greenwood for a much shorter commute (~15 min), Center Grove schools, and every major shop and restaurant nearby. Choose Franklin if value matters most — homes start around the $180s versus Greenwood's $250s — and you want an authentic courthouse-square town with a college. The tradeoff is roughly double the drive time.

Greenwood and Franklin are the two anchors of Johnson County, and they present one of the clearest tradeoffs in the metro: convenience versus value. Greenwood puts you 15 minutes from downtown with every amenity at hand. Franklin asks for roughly double the drive and hands back a meaningfully lower price per home.

Unlike some suburb matchups where the differences are subtle, this one is stark — which makes it easier to decide.

Price: Franklin Is Substantially More Affordable

This is the headline difference. Franklin homes typically run from the $180s for smaller starter and older properties to the upper $340s for updated or newer construction. Greenwood generally starts around the $250s and reaches the $450s.

That gap is large enough to change what kind of home you can buy. A budget that gets you a modest starter in Greenwood may buy a considerably larger or more updated home in Franklin. For first-time buyers and anyone stretching, Franklin deserves a serious look. Compare our Greenwood and Franklin pages.

Commute: Greenwood's Clear Advantage

Greenwood sits roughly 15 minutes from downtown Indianapolis via I-65 and US-31 — one of the shortest suburban commutes in the metro. Franklin runs about 30 minutes via SR-44 and I-65.

Fifteen extra minutes each way is about two and a half hours a week. For some buyers that's an easy trade for a bigger house; for others it's the whole decision. Be honest with yourself about which you are.

Amenities vs Authenticity

Greenwood is the south side's retail and restaurant hub. The US-31 corridor carries virtually every major national chain and retailer, while the Main Street corridor to the north preserves some historic character. If you want everything within ten minutes, Greenwood delivers.

Franklin's strength is authenticity. Its downtown courthouse square is a living, walkable community core that has served Johnson County for generations — not a redevelopment project. Franklin College adds cultural programming, athletics, and community engagement that a town of 25,000 would not otherwise support. It reads as a real small town, because it is one.

Schools

Greenwood-area buyers are frequently drawn by Center Grove Schools, one of the more sought-after districts on the south side and a genuine driver of local home values. Franklin Community Schools serves Franklin and is well-regarded locally. If a specific district is your priority, note that Greenwood addresses fall across more than one district — worth confirming attendance zones before you make an offer on any given house.

Choose Greenwood If…

  • A short commute is a priority
  • You want Center Grove schools (confirm the attendance zone)
  • You want retail, dining, and services minutes away
  • You'd rather have convenience than extra square footage

Choose Franklin If…

  • Value is your top priority — your budget goes noticeably further
  • You want an authentic small-town square and college-town energy
  • You're comfortable with a ~30 minute drive downtown
  • You're a first-time buyer looking for an achievable entry point

The Honest Verdict

If you commute downtown daily and want amenities at hand, Greenwood is worth its premium. If you work from home, work on the south side, or simply want the most house for your money in Johnson County, Franklin is one of the better values in the metro — and the courthouse square is genuinely charming.

Buyers who assume they must be in Greenwood are often surprised by what the same money buys 15 minutes farther south.

Frequently Asked Questions — Greenwood vs Franklin

Is Greenwood or Franklin more affordable?

Franklin is meaningfully more affordable. Franklin homes typically run from the $180s to the upper $340s, while Greenwood generally runs from the $250s to the $450s. The gap is large enough that the same budget often buys a noticeably larger or more updated home in Franklin.

How far is Franklin from downtown Indianapolis?

Franklin is roughly 30 minutes from downtown Indianapolis via SR-44 and I-65, compared with about 15 minutes from Greenwood. That extra 15 minutes each way is the main tradeoff for Franklin's lower home prices.

Is Greenwood in the Center Grove school district?

Parts of the Greenwood area are served by Center Grove Schools, which is a major driver of local demand, but Greenwood addresses fall across more than one district. Always confirm the attendance zone for a specific address before making an offer.

Is Franklin Indiana a good place to live?

Yes, particularly for buyers who value affordability and small-town character. Franklin is the Johnson County seat, with a genuine walkable courthouse square, Franklin College adding cultural and athletic programming, and some of the most attractive home values in the county.
