Boone County sits northwest of Indianapolis, bordered by Hendricks County to the south and Hamilton County to the east. The county blends luxury suburban living in Zionsville — consistently ranked among Indiana's best places to live — with one of the state's fastest-growing new communities in Whitestown, and a more affordable, small-city feel in the county seat of Lebanon. Growth along the I-65 corridor has accelerated significantly over the past decade, drawing buyers from across the metro who want more space, newer construction, and access to highly regarded schools without the premium prices of Hamilton County's core cities. Thorntown and Jamestown add a true rural small-town character to the county's mix, offering larger lots and a slower pace of life still within a reasonable drive of Indianapolis. Boone County Real Estate Market

Boone County offers one of the widest price spectrums of any county in the Indianapolis metro area — which is precisely what makes it attractive to such a broad range of buyers. Zionsville anchors the upper end of the market, with home prices typically ranging from the $400s to well over $1M. Luxury custom homes, historic brick-street properties in the village, and large estate lots define much of Zionsville's inventory. Whitestown, meanwhile, has emerged as one of Indiana's fastest-growing communities, where buyers find newly built homes in the $280s to $450s with modern floorplans and neighborhood amenities. Lebanon, the county seat, offers genuine affordability — homes here typically range from the $180s to the $320s, making it an attractive option for first-time buyers and investors. Thorntown and Jamestown reflect true rural character, with older homes, larger parcels, and lower price points. Near the I-65 corridor, competitive demand has tightened inventory, and well-priced homes often move quickly. Why Buy a Home in Boone County?

School districts are one of the most compelling reasons buyers choose Boone County. Zionsville Community Schools is consistently ranked among Indiana's highest-performing districts, drawing families from across the metro who prioritize education. Whitestown buyers may fall within Western Boone or Zionsville school district boundaries depending on their location, so it's worth confirming district assignment when searching. Lebanon Community Schools serves the county seat and surrounding areas.

Quality of life across Boone County is exceptional. Zionsville's SullivanMunce Cultural Center, brick-street downtown lined with local shops and restaurants, and extensive trail system make it one of Central Indiana's most walkable and livable communities. Eagle Creek Park — one of the largest city parks in the U.S. — sits just over the county line and provides easy access to hiking, sailing, and outdoor recreation for Boone County residents. The Boone County 4-H Fairgrounds anchors a strong agricultural heritage and community spirit across the county's smaller towns. Hamilton County's Grand Park sports complex is also within easy reach for youth athletics. I-65 provides a direct and efficient commute corridor to downtown Indianapolis and north toward Lafayette, and US-421 connects communities throughout the county. For buyers comparing Boone County to Hamilton County, many Boone communities offer comparable school quality and lifestyle at a more accessible price point — especially in Whitestown and Lebanon.
