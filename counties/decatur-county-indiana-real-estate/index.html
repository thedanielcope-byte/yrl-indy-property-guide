Decatur County Indiana Real Estate

Anchored by Greensburg — the historic small city famous for the tree growing from its courthouse tower. Affordable homes and farmland southeast of Indianapolis along I-74.

📍 SE of Indianapolis on I-74 🏙 County Seat: Greensburg 🏡 $150s – $300s ~50 min to Downtown Indy

Decatur County sits about 50 miles southeast of Indianapolis along the I-74 corridor, anchored by Greensburg — a historic small city of around 12,000 best known for a curious civic landmark: a tree growing from the top of the county courthouse tower, a quirk that has drawn visitors for more than a century. With a county population of roughly 26,000, Decatur County combines a strong agricultural base with a manufacturing economy that includes a Honda Manufacturing of Indiana plant near Greensburg, one of the largest employers in the region. The county offers a much lower cost of living than the Indianapolis metro, easy interstate access for commuters, and the kind of working-town character that's increasingly rare. Westport and several small towns and townships round out the rest of the county. Your Realty Link's team is happy to help buyers and sellers throughout Decatur County.

Decatur County Real Estate Market

The Decatur County real estate market is one of the more affordable in the broader Indianapolis region. Homes typically range from the $150s for established single-family homes and starter properties in Greensburg and the rural towns to the $300s for larger or updated homes and properties with acreage. Greensburg itself offers a varied mix — historic homes near the courthouse square, mid-century neighborhoods, and newer subdivisions on the edges of town. The presence of the Honda manufacturing plant and associated suppliers brings a steady flow of workforce buyers to the market, which supports demand for entry-level and mid-range homes. Rural parts of the county add farmland, country homes, and properties with land at very accessible prices.

Why Buy a Home in Decatur County?

Decatur County's appeal is rooted in affordability, employment, and small-town character. Greensburg's downtown square — anchored by the famous tree-topped courthouse — is genuinely worth walking, with locally owned restaurants, shops, and a strong community calendar of events. The Honda plant and related suppliers provide a stable employment base, and easy I-74 access means commuting to Indianapolis or Columbus is realistic for many families. Decatur County is also home to a network of parks and recreation, including Westport Park and a number of small lakes and recreation areas. School districts in the county include Greensburg Community Schools, North Decatur Community Schools, and South Decatur Community Schools. For buyers seeking real value, an employment base, and small-town life within easy reach of the metro, Decatur County is worth a look.
