Hancock County Indiana Real Estate

Just east of Indianapolis, Hancock County is one of Central Indiana's fastest-growing markets — combining suburban growth in McCordsville and New Palestine with small-town character in Greenfield and Fortville.

📍 East of Indianapolis 🏙 6 Cities & Towns 🏡 $150s – $500s Growing Schools & Community

Hancock County stretches east of Indianapolis and Marion County, accessible via I-70, US-40, and SR-67. Once largely agricultural, the county has experienced dramatic residential growth over the past decade — particularly in the northern corridor near Hamilton County. McCordsville has emerged as one of the region's premier suburban destinations, with master-planned communities and newer construction drawing buyers from across the metro. Greenfield, the county seat, maintains a vibrant small-city downtown and offers a more affordable alternative to the northern suburbs. New Palestine and Fortville provide a blend of established neighborhoods and new development with strong school district reputations. Cumberland straddles the Marion/Hancock county line, offering easy access to Indianapolis and moderate price points. Hancock County Real Estate Market

McCordsville and New Palestine are competitive seller's markets with new construction driving significant activity — homes in these corridors typically range from the $280s to the low $500s. Greenfield and Fortville offer a wider variety, with existing homes and some new construction in the $180s to $360s. Cumberland's proximity to Indianapolis keeps demand steady, with homes commonly priced in the $170s to $280s. Rural pockets like Shirley and eastern Hancock County offer larger lots and older homes in more affordable ranges.

The county as a whole benefits from lower property taxes compared to Marion County while maintaining easy metro access — a compelling combination for buyers priced out of Hamilton County or looking to stretch their budget without sacrificing convenience. Inventory in the northern growth corridor remains competitive, with multiple-offer situations common on well-priced new construction. Working with an experienced MIBOR agent is essential to navigating these conditions successfully.

Why Buy a Home in Hancock County?

Three school districts serve Hancock County, each with strong community reputations. Mt. Vernon Community Schools — serving McCordsville and Fortville — has seen significant investment in new facilities to accommodate rapid enrollment growth. Greenfield-Central Community Schools anchors the county seat and offers a well-rounded program from elementary through high school. Eastern Hancock Community Schools serves New Palestine and surrounding rural areas with a tight-knit, highly regarded academic and athletic program.

Beyond schools, Hancock County appeals to buyers who want suburban convenience at prices meaningfully below Hamilton County. Easy access to I-70 puts downtown Indianapolis within 20 to 40 minutes from most areas of the county. The B&O Trail through Greenfield, Pennsy Trail connectivity, and the Brandywine Conservation Area offer outdoor recreation for residents of all ages. New retail and restaurant development in the McCordsville corridor — including proximity to Geist-area amenities just to the north — continues to enhance the county's appeal for families relocating to the region. For buyers seeking a balance of affordability, growth, and quality of life, Hancock County is one of Central Indiana's most compelling options.
