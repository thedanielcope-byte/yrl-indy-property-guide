Hendricks County Indiana Homes for Sale

One of Indiana's fastest-growing counties — Avon, Brownsburg, Plainfield, and Danville offer outstanding schools, easy highway access, and competitive pricing west of Indianapolis. Your Realty Link serves buyers and sellers throughout all of Hendricks County.

📍 West of Indianapolis 🏙 4 Major Cities 🏡 $230s – $450s I-70 & I-74 Corridors

Hendricks County sits directly west of Indianapolis and has earned a well-deserved reputation as one of the Indianapolis metro's most dynamic growth markets. Spanning approximately 408 square miles, the county is bisected by two of Indiana's most important interstates — I-70 and I-74 — making it exceptionally well-connected to both downtown Indianapolis and the broader Midwest. That highway access, combined with proximity to Indianapolis International Airport and a strong inventory of housing at prices below Hamilton County, has fueled sustained population growth that now exceeds 175,000 residents. Cities like Avon, Brownsburg, and Plainfield each have their own strong identity and loyal community base, while Danville carries the charm of a classic Indiana county seat with a small-town square and a pace that contrasts pleasantly with the faster-growing communities to the east. For buyers who want more home for their budget without sacrificing convenience, Hendricks County is one of the most compelling options in all of Central Indiana. Hendricks County Real Estate Market

The Hendricks County market has been defined by persistent demand and steady appreciation driven by the county's growth fundamentals. Avon's real estate market is perhaps the most competitive in the county, bolstered by strong buyer interest in Avon Community Schools and a well-developed suburban infrastructure of parks, trails, and retail. Brownsburg draws a devoted following of its own — the community has a distinct identity anchored in part by its motorsports heritage at Lucas Oil Raceway, and homes there tend to move quickly due to community loyalty and school quality. Plainfield occupies a different niche: its location near Indianapolis International Airport and the major logistics and distribution corridor along I-70 creates a pragmatic appeal for buyers who prioritize commute options and employment proximity. Home styles across the county vary from newer builds in planned subdivisions to established ranch homes on larger lots and custom construction in more rural settings. Danville offers some of the county's most affordable pricing, making it a practical entry point into Hendricks County homeownership for budget-conscious buyers.

Why Buy a Home in Hendricks County?

Hendricks County's growth over the past two decades hasn't happened by accident — it reflects a set of advantages that buyers consistently prioritize. Four strong school districts anchor four distinct communities: Avon Community Schools, Brownsburg Community Schools, Plainfield Community Schools, and Danville Community Schools each maintain dedicated followings and strong community involvement. Highway access is among the best in the metro — I-70 and I-74 both run through the county, and US-36 and US-40 provide additional east-west connectivity, making commutes to Indianapolis, the airport, and points beyond genuinely manageable. The county's proximity to Indianapolis International Airport is a practical advantage for frequent travelers and a key employment driver for the logistics and distribution jobs centered in the Plainfield corridor. Compared to Hamilton County to the north, Hendricks County consistently offers more square footage and larger lots at lower price points — a trade-off that appeals strongly to buyers weighing value. The Indiana Premium Outlets in Plainfield and a growing retail and dining scene in Avon and Brownsburg mean that daily convenience has caught up with the county's residential growth. For buyers relocating from out of state, Hendricks County is frequently a top recommendation from Your Realty Link agents as one of the metro's best-kept value secrets.
