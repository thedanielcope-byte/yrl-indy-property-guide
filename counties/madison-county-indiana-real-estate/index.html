Madison County Indiana Real Estate

Just northeast of Indianapolis along the I-69 corridor, Madison County offers affordable homes, small-town living, and easy access to the metro. Your Realty Link serves buyers and sellers throughout the county.

📍 NE of Indianapolis on I-69 🏙 County Seat: Anderson 🏡 $150s – $300s ~35–45 min to Downtown Indy

Madison County sits just northeast of the Indianapolis metro, anchored by Anderson — the county seat and largest city — along the I-69 corridor that connects Indianapolis to Fort Wayne. With a population of roughly 130,000, the county blends a historic industrial heritage with a quieter, more affordable suburban and small-town character that appeals to buyers priced out of Hamilton County. Madison County is home to Anderson University, Hoosier Park Racing & Casino, and a network of parks, lakes, and recreational areas, while neighboring communities like Pendleton, Lapel, Alexandria, and Elwood each bring their own personality. For buyers seeking more home and more land for the money — without giving up easy highway access to Indianapolis — Madison County is one of the metro's best-kept secrets. Madison County Real Estate Market

The Madison County real estate market is one of the most affordable in the Indianapolis metro. Homes typically range from the mid-$100s for established single-family homes and starter properties in Anderson and Elwood to the $300s for newer construction and larger homes in Pendleton and Lapel, which have benefited from their proximity to Hamilton County and direct I-69 access. The county offers a wide mix of housing styles — historic homes in established neighborhoods, mid-century ranches, modern subdivisions, and rural properties with acreage. For first-time buyers and value-focused families, Madison County delivers more home and more lot than comparable Hamilton County addresses. Investors also find a healthy supply of rental candidates and renovation opportunities at price points that support strong returns.

Why Buy a Home in Madison County?

Madison County's biggest draw is value — but it offers far more than just affordable prices. The county has invested in revitalizing its downtowns, parks, and trails, and it remains home to important employers, educational institutions, and recreation. Anderson is anchored by Anderson University, Hoosier Park, and a downtown that's seen real reinvestment. Pendleton and Lapel offer well-regarded school districts and easy highway access for Indianapolis commuters. Mounds State Park along the White River provides hiking, history, and family recreation. School districts across the county include Anderson Community Schools, South Madison Community Schools (Pendleton), Frankton-Lapel Community Schools, Alexandria Community Schools, and Elwood Community Schools — each with its own strengths. Compared with metros further from Indianapolis, Madison County is a strong choice for buyers who want affordability without isolation.
