Step 1: Understand Your Budget

Before you start touring homes, get a clear picture of what you can realistically afford. This means more than just a monthly payment — it includes property taxes, homeowner's insurance, potential HOA fees, and maintenance reserves. Use the Your Realty Link mortgage calculator to run different scenarios, then connect with a local lender for a formal pre-approval.

Pre-approval is not just a formality. In Indianapolis's competitive neighborhoods, a pre-approval letter signals to sellers that you're a serious buyer — and it can make the difference between winning and losing a home you love.

Step 2: Get Pre-Approved

A mortgage pre-approval gives you a specific loan amount you qualify for, based on your income, credit, and debts. It also helps you understand your loan options. First-time buyers in Indiana commonly use:

  • FHA Loans: As little as 3.5% down, more flexible credit requirements. Popular with first-time buyers. Learn more about FHA loans in Indianapolis.
  • Conventional Loans: 3–5% down for qualified buyers, no upfront mortgage insurance premium.
  • VA Loans: Zero down payment for eligible veterans and active-duty military. Learn more about VA loans in Indiana.
  • USDA Loans: Zero down for eligible buyers in qualifying rural areas of Indiana.
  • Indiana Down Payment Assistance: IHCDA programs can help cover down payment and closing costs for qualifying buyers.

Step 3: Choose the Right Neighborhood

Indianapolis has dozens of neighborhoods, each with a distinct character and price range. Here's a quick guide for first-time buyers by priority:

  • Best value: Lawrence, Beech Grove, Southport — homes in the $150s–$300s
  • Great schools, good value: Greenwood, Avon, Brownsburg — homes in the $250s–$400s
  • Urban lifestyle: Broad Ripple, Irvington, Fountain Square — walkable, character-filled, varied price points
  • New construction: Westfield, McCordsville, Whitestown area — newer builds in growing communities

Down Payment Assistance in Indiana

If saving for a down payment has been the biggest obstacle, Indiana has programs that can help. The Indiana Housing and Community Development Authority (IHCDA) offers down payment assistance and mortgage credit certificates for qualifying first-time buyers. Eligibility is based on income limits, purchase price caps, and the location of the property. Learn more about down payment assistance programs in Indiana.

Frequently Asked Questions — First Time Buyers

How much do I need for a down payment in Indianapolis?

Down payment requirements vary by loan type. FHA loans require as little as 3.5% down, conventional loans can be as low as 3–5%, and VA and USDA loans may require zero down for qualified buyers. Indiana also has down payment assistance programs that can help cover upfront costs. Your Realty Link can connect you with trusted local lenders.

What are the best neighborhoods for first-time buyers in Indianapolis?

First-time buyers often find great value in neighborhoods like Greenwood, Avon, Brownsburg, Lawrence, and Beech Grove — areas with solid schools, good commute access, and homes typically in the $150s–$300s range. Your Realty Link helps buyers identify the right fit based on budget and lifestyle priorities.

Are there down payment assistance programs in Indiana?

Yes. Indiana Housing (IHCDA) offers down payment assistance and mortgage credit certificate programs for qualifying buyers. Eligibility depends on income, purchase price, and property location. Contact Your Realty Link to learn more and get connected with a lender who works with these programs.
