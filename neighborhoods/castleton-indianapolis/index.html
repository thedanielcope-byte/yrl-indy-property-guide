Castleton Indianapolis & Homes for Sale

One of Indianapolis's most convenient northeast-side neighborhoods — established homes, top retail, and easy I-465 and I-69 access. Your Realty Link helps buyers and sellers throughout Castleton.

📍 Northeast Indianapolis 🛍 Castleton Square Retail Hub 🏡 $200s – $400s+ ~20 min to Downtown Indy

Castleton is one of Indianapolis's most convenient northeast-side neighborhoods — a residential and commercial area built around the intersection of I-465 and I-69, anchored by Castleton Square Mall and the metro's largest concentration of north-side retail, dining, and services. The neighborhood mixes established single-family residential streets, well-known apartment and condo communities, and the constant activity of one of Indianapolis's most-trafficked commercial corridors. For buyers, Castleton offers an unusually practical combination: established homes at attainable prices, virtually every chain and many independent restaurants within minutes, and direct interstate access to north-side employers and downtown. Your Realty Link helps buyers and sellers throughout Castleton with full MIBOR MLS access and a hands-on, client-first approach.

Living in Castleton

Daily life in Castleton is shaped by an enormous concentration of convenience. Castleton Square Mall and the surrounding commercial corridor along East 82nd Street and Allisonville Road put just about every category of shopping and dining within a five-minute drive. The neighborhood also includes substantial green space — Skiles Test Nature Park, several smaller parks, and the nearby Geist Reservoir trails — that provides a meaningful counterbalance to the commercial intensity. Residentially, Castleton offers an unusually broad range, from older established subdivisions with mature trees and well-maintained homes to newer townhome and condo communities. The 82nd Street and I-69 corridors place much of the north-side employment base within a short commute. For buyers who prioritize practical convenience and want a wide range of residential options in one neighborhood, Castleton delivers.

Castleton Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Castleton market is shaped by the diversity of its housing stock. Homes typically range from the $200s for established single-family homes, townhomes, and condos to the $400s and higher for larger or updated properties in well-regarded subdivisions. Townhomes and condos near the I-69 corridor offer accessible entry points for first-time buyers and downsizers, while established single-family neighborhoods along the 82nd Street corridor and north of it provide mid-range options that have historically held value well. The combination of convenient location, steady demand from a wide range of buyer types, and a deep rental tenant pool also makes Castleton an active market for investors. Inventory turns over consistently across price points.

Schools in Castleton

Castleton straddles two well-regarded Indianapolis township school districts. Most of the area east of Allisonville Road is served by the MSD of Lawrence Township, known for its diverse programming and International Baccalaureate offerings. Areas west of Allisonville Road typically fall within the MSD of Washington Township, also well regarded for its academic and extracurricular programs. Because the school assignment depends on the property's exact address, a Your Realty Link agent can help you understand which district serves any home you're considering and recommend confirming current details directly with the district. Families researching schools in the Castleton area should plan to verify boundaries early in their search.

Commute from Castleton to Indianapolis

Castleton's location is one of its most cited advantages. Downtown Indianapolis is roughly 10 miles south — about a 20-minute drive via I-465 to I-65 or via Keystone Avenue, depending on traffic. The Fishers and Hamilton County employment corridor along I-69 is approximately 15 minutes north, putting the metro's two largest north-side job markets within easy reach in both directions. The 82nd Street and Allisonville Road corridors also connect Castleton to nearby employment centers in Lawrence and Geist. For buyers prioritizing flexibility in their commute and access to north-side amenities, Castleton is consistently one of the most practical choices in the city.
