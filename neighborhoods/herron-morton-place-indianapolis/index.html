Herron-Morton Place is one of the most architecturally impressive neighborhoods on the near-north side of Indianapolis. Bounded roughly by 16th and 22nd streets just above downtown, this historic district takes its name from the former Herron School of Art and the old Morton School, and its streets are lined with some of the grandest residential architecture in the city — towering Queen Anne Victorians, American Four Squares, and stately brick mansions set on generous lots. Once at risk of decline, the neighborhood was rescued by preservation-minded residents and today stands as a showcase of restored period homes with mature tree canopies overhead. Its proximity to Mass Ave, downtown, and the arts community makes it a favorite among buyers who want scale, character, and walkability. Living in Herron-Morton Place

Herron-Morton Place blends grandeur with genuine neighborliness. Residents take pride in their historic homes, and the community is known for its preservation ethic, active association, and gathering spots like the neighborhood's central park and the Tinker House, a restored complex that hosts events and creative businesses. The former Herron art campus gives the district an enduring cultural identity, and galleries, studios, and independent restaurants are woven throughout the surrounding near-north area. Mass Ave's dining and entertainment are a short walk or bike ride south, while the shops and cafés along College and Central avenues serve everyday needs. Wide sidewalks, deep front porches, and a walkable grid encourage the kind of front-yard conversations and evening strolls that define established urban neighborhoods. It's a place where architecture, art, and community reinforce one another.

Herron-Morton Place Home Prices & Real Estate Market

Herron-Morton Place sits at the higher end of the near-north market thanks to its exceptional housing stock. Smaller homes, carriage houses, and condos typically start in the $300s, while the restored Victorians, Four Squares, and larger historic homes the district is known for generally range from the $400s into the $700s and above. Fully renovated showcase mansions on prime blocks can command premium prices. Because these are older, often large homes, condition and quality of restoration vary widely — from lovingly maintained gems to properties still awaiting their turn. Inventory is limited and preservation guidelines shape what buyers and sellers can do, so working with an agent who understands historic homes, their systems, and their value is especially important in this neighborhood.

Schools in Herron-Morton Place

Herron-Morton Place is served by Indianapolis Public Schools, and families in this near-north district have access to a range of IPS magnet programs, charter schools, and private institutions nearby. Several elementary, middle, and high school options serve the area, and IPS choice enrollment allows families to apply beyond their assigned attendance boundary. Because school zones and choice policies change from year to year, we recommend confirming current details with the district for any specific address. Commute from Herron-Morton Place to Downtown Indianapolis

Downtown Indianapolis is only about two miles south of Herron-Morton Place — typically a 10-minute drive via Delaware, Pennsylvania, or Central Avenue. The neighborhood's near-north position keeps Monument Circle, the sports and convention district, and downtown employers within quick reach. IndyGo's Red Line runs nearby along College Avenue, offering a car-free route into the city center, and the flat, connected grid makes cycling and walking to Mass Ave and downtown genuinely practical. For buyers who want a stately historic home close to the action, the commute here is short and flexible.
