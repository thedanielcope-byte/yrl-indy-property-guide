Nora is a well-established community on the far north side of Indianapolis, centered near the busy Keystone Avenue and 86th Street corridor. Once a separate town, Nora today is known for its mature, wooded lots, mid-century and ranch-style homes, and easy access to some of the metro's best shopping and dining at Nora Plaza, the Fashion Mall, and the surrounding retail hubs. The area sits within the Metropolitan School District of Washington Township, a major draw for families. With larger lots, tall trees, and a settled, residential feel, Nora offers a more spacious alternative to the denser neighborhoods closer to downtown while keeping shopping and highways minutes away. Living in Nora

Living in Nora combines suburban comfort with north-side convenience. The area is anchored by everyday amenities — grocery stores, restaurants, and the shops at Nora Plaza — while the upscale Fashion Mall at Keystone and the Monon Trail's northern stretch sit just minutes away. Residential streets are quiet and green, with many homes set on generous, tree-shaded lots that give the neighborhood a park-like feel. Nora's established character appeals to families, downsizers, and buyers who want space and mature landscaping without moving far from the city. Parks, the White River, and nearby Broad Ripple add recreation and dining options. It's a practical, comfortable place to live where errands are easy, the trees are tall, and downtown remains a short drive south.

Nora Home Prices & Real Estate Market

Nora's housing stock is largely made up of mid-century and ranch-style homes, along with some larger custom houses and newer builds, and pricing reflects that variety. Smaller or original-condition ranches typically start in the $250s, while updated homes and larger properties on desirable wooded lots generally range into the $400s to $500s. The area's most spacious custom homes closer to the river or on premium lots can reach the $600s. Larger lot sizes, mature trees, and Washington Township schools all support values here. Because homes range widely in size, age, and condition, local guidance helps buyers and sellers set accurate expectations. Well-maintained, updated homes in Nora continue to see steady demand from north-side buyers.

Schools in Nora

Nora is served by the Metropolitan School District of Washington Township, one of the area's well-known public school systems and a major reason many families choose the neighborhood. The district serves students from elementary through high school, and the area also offers several private and independent school options nearby. Because attendance boundaries and program offerings can change over time, we recommend confirming current details with the district for any specific address. A Your Realty Link agent can help you understand which schools serve each home you're considering in Nora.

Commute from Nora to Downtown Indianapolis

Nora's location makes commuting straightforward. Downtown Indianapolis sits about ten miles south — roughly a 20-minute drive via Keystone Avenue (SR-431) or Meridian Street (US-31). Interstate 465 runs just north of the area, connecting drivers quickly to the entire metro loop, the north-side employment centers, and Carmel. The 86th Street and Keystone corridors keep shopping and services close, and the Monon Trail offers a car-free route south. For buyers who want quick highway access and a short trip to the north-side job hubs, Nora is well positioned.
