The Old Northside is one of Indianapolis's most prestigious historic districts — a near-north enclave of grand Victorian mansions, elegant carriage houses, and meticulously restored period homes just steps from downtown and the Mass Ave cultural district. Developed in the late 1800s as the address of choice for the city's leading families, the neighborhood fell into decline in the mid-20th century before a dedicated wave of preservationists brought its architecture back to life. Today its brick streets are lined with restored Italianate, Queen Anne, and Romanesque homes, many listed on the National Register of Historic Places. Buyers here are drawn to the craftsmanship, the walkable location, and the rare chance to own a piece of Indianapolis history. Living in the Old Northside

Life in the Old Northside blends historic grandeur with urban convenience. Residents walk to Mass Ave's theaters, restaurants, galleries, and coffee shops, and downtown's offices, sports venues, and the Cultural Trail are all within easy reach. The neighborhood itself is a walkable showcase of Victorian architecture, with pocket parks, the nearby President Benjamin Harrison Home, and a calendar of home tours and neighborhood events that reflect a deeply engaged community. Front porches, ornate ironwork, and mature street trees give the district a distinctive, storybook character. It's a neighborhood that appeals to people who love architecture, history, and being able to leave the car at home. The Old Northside offers a genuine downtown-adjacent lifestyle wrapped in some of the finest historic homes in the state.

Old Northside Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Old Northside sits at the higher end of the near-downtown market, reflecting the scale and craftsmanship of its historic homes. Condos, smaller cottages, and homes needing work can start in the $350s, while restored single-family Victorians generally range from the $500s into the $700s. The neighborhood's largest and most fully restored mansions — with original millwork, updated systems, and premium locations — can reach the $800s and well beyond. Because these are one-of-a-kind historic properties, values depend heavily on the quality of restoration, square footage, and lot. Demand for authentic, move-in-ready historic homes near downtown remains strong. Buying or selling here calls for an agent who understands how to position and value distinctive period properties.

Schools in the Old Northside

The Old Northside is served by Indianapolis Public Schools, with several IPS magnet programs, charter schools, and private options available throughout the downtown and near-north area. The district's central location means a variety of school choices are within a short distance. Because attendance boundaries and enrollment-choice programs can change from year to year, we recommend confirming current details with the district for any specific address. A Your Realty Link agent can help you understand which schools and programs serve each home you're considering in the Old Northside.

Commute from the Old Northside to Downtown Indianapolis

Few Indianapolis neighborhoods offer a shorter commute than the Old Northside. Downtown is essentially next door — a five-minute drive or an easy walk or bike ride south via Delaware, Central, or Pennsylvania Street. Interstates 65 and 70 border the area, connecting residents quickly to the airport and the wider metro, and the Indianapolis Cultural Trail links the neighborhood to downtown destinations on foot or by bike. For buyers who work downtown or value walkable access to the city center, the Old Northside is about as convenient as it gets.
