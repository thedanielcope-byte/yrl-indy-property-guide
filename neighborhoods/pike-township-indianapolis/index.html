Pike Township covers a substantial portion of Indianapolis's northwest side — a residential and increasingly mixed-use area built around the I-65 corridor and anchored by Eagle Creek Park, one of the largest municipal parks in the United States. The township stretches from the Marion-Hamilton County line south to the airport corridor and includes a remarkable mix of established single-family subdivisions, newer townhome and condo communities, the commercial corridor along Lafayette Road and 86th Street, and substantial green space. For buyers, Pike Township offers a combination that has historically been underrated: established homes at attainable prices, access to one of the country's great urban parks, and a convenient location for both downtown commuters and frequent travelers using Indianapolis International Airport. Living in Pike Township

Life in Pike Township is shaped by an unusually strong balance of nature and convenience. Eagle Creek Park — roughly 3,900 acres of land and 1,400 acres of water — anchors the western part of the township and gives residents access to hiking trails, mountain biking, a swimming beach, sailing and kayaking on the reservoir, a marina, an ornithology center, a treetop adventure course, and seasonal events that draw visitors from across the metro. Beyond the park, the township offers the steady conveniences of an established residential area: shopping and dining along 86th Street and Michigan Road, multiple grocery options, and a network of community-serving schools and parks. The township's mix of neighborhood styles — older established subdivisions with mature trees, newer townhome communities, and condo developments — gives buyers genuine range. For buyers who want green space, value, and practical access to both downtown and the airport, Pike Township is one of the strongest options on the northwest side.

Pike Township Home Prices & Real Estate Market

The Pike Township market offers some of the better value on Indianapolis's north and northwest sides. Homes typically range from the $200s for established single-family homes, townhomes, and condos to the $400s and higher for larger or updated homes and properties on wooded lots near Eagle Creek Park. Townhomes and condos near the I-465 corridor offer attainable entry points for first-time buyers and investors, while established single-family neighborhoods along 86th Street and north of it provide mid-range options with mature landscaping and quiet streets. The combination of accessible prices, convenient location, and steady rental demand also makes Pike Township an active market for investors, including those evaluating multi-family properties. Inventory moves consistently across price points when homes are well-prepared and accurately priced.

Schools in Pike Township

Pike Township is served primarily by the Metropolitan School District of Pike Township, which operates the area's public elementary and middle schools and Pike High School. The district is known for its diverse student population, broad academic and career-technical programming, and consistent investment in facilities. The township also offers access to a range of private and charter school options popular with northwest-side families. Because attendance boundaries vary by address and school-choice programs can change, a Your Realty Link agent can help you understand which schools serve any home you're considering and recommend verifying current details with the district.

Commute from Pike Township to Indianapolis

Pike Township's commute math is particularly strong. Downtown Indianapolis is roughly 10 miles southeast — about a 20-minute drive via I-65, which runs along the township's eastern edge, or I-465 for cross-town access. Indianapolis International Airport is only about 15 minutes south via I-465, making Pike Township especially attractive to frequent travelers, airport-area workers, and anyone working in the airport logistics and distribution corridor. Major employment centers along the I-65 and I-465 corridors are within easy reach, and the township's broader access to north-side employment via 86th Street and Michigan Road adds further flexibility.
