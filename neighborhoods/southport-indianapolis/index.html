Southport is one of those Indianapolis neighborhoods that long-time residents know well but newcomers tend to discover by accident — and then stay for good. Technically an independent city within Marion County, Southport occupies a quiet residential stretch on the south side of Indianapolis along Southport Road, roughly halfway between downtown Indianapolis and the shopping hub of Greenwood. The area retains a small-town feel despite sitting inside the I-465 loop, with its own city government, police department, and a neighborhood identity that stretches back over a century. Homes here are predominantly ranch-style and traditional two-stories built from the 1950s through 1990s, with scattered new construction filling in as the area continues to evolve. Living in Southport

Southport's appeal is practical and genuine. The neighborhood is residential, quiet, and well-connected — residents reach downtown Indianapolis in about 15 minutes via Madison Avenue or I-65, and Greenwood's shopping, dining, and entertainment along US-31 is an equally short drive south. The immediate area offers grocery stores, restaurants, churches, and small businesses along the Southport Road corridor. Parks and recreational facilities serve the community through Perry Township's park system, and the neighborhood's flat, tree-lined streets are easy to walk. For buyers who prioritize value, a low-stress commute, and a neighborhood where people actually know their neighbors, Southport consistently delivers. It is also a natural landing spot for first-time buyers priced out of the north side and investors looking for strong rental demand in a well-located area.

Southport Home Prices & Real Estate Market

Southport is one of the most affordable residential pockets in Marion County, and that affordability is a major draw for first-time buyers, growing families, and investors. Homes typically range from the low $130s for older homes or those needing updates to the mid $280s for fully renovated or newer-construction properties. The bulk of the market — solid ranch homes, split-levels, and two-stories in established neighborhoods — falls in the $170s to $240s. South-side demand has strengthened in recent years as buyers who are priced out of Hamilton County and the north side discover the value proposition. Well-maintained homes in good school zones move steadily, and the area's proximity to Greenwood adds employment and retail convenience.

Schools in Southport

Southport is served primarily by Perry Township Schools, one of the largest school districts in Marion County. The district includes Southport High School and a network of elementary and middle schools serving the south-side community. Perry Township is known for its diversity and for programs that serve a wide range of student needs. Families also have access to charter and private school options within a reasonable drive. Because attendance areas can vary by address, a Your Realty Link agent can help you confirm which specific schools serve any property you are considering.

Commute from Southport to Downtown Indianapolis

Southport's location makes for a straightforward commute in either direction. Downtown Indianapolis is roughly eight miles north — about a 15-minute drive via Madison Avenue, East Street, or I-65. Greenwood sits about five miles south along US-31. I-465 is close for cross-town access, and the south-side corridor connects to major employers at the Indianapolis International Airport, along the I-65 corridor, and throughout the Greenwood commercial district. For buyers who split their commute between downtown and the south suburbs, Southport hits the geographic sweet spot.
