Central Indiana is one of the most active new construction markets in the Midwest, and much of that growth is happening in master-planned communities on the edges of the metro. These neighborhoods offer something resale can't: a brand-new home with a modern open floor plan, current energy efficiency, a builder warranty, and — depending on how early you buy — the ability to choose your lot, layout, and finishes. Communities range from townhome and starter neighborhoods to move-up and semi-custom enclaves, often with shared amenities like pools, trails, and clubhouses. The trade-off is that the on-site agent works for the builder. Your Realty Link is an Indianapolis-based MIBOR brokerage that represents buyers in new construction across Central Indiana — at no cost to you — and Broker Daniel Cope helps clients choose the right community, negotiate, and close with confidence.

Where to Find New Construction Communities in Central Indiana

New neighborhoods follow the available land, which means the growth suburbs lead the way. Hamilton County is the epicenter — Westfield, Noblesville, and Fishers all have active master-planned communities across a wide range of price points. To the east, McCordsville is one of the fastest-growing new-home markets in the region. West of the city, Whitestown in Boone County continues to expand rapidly. For builder-specific guidance and county-by-county options, see our service pages on new construction and new construction by county, or the broader Indianapolis homes for sale hub.

New Construction Home Prices in Central Indiana

New construction pricing depends on the builder, community, and how much you customize. Townhomes and entry-level single-family plans typically start in the $300s, giving first-time and value-focused buyers a brand-new option. The core of the market — move-up single-family homes with three to four bedrooms, flexible floor plans, and popular upgrades — generally falls in the $350s to $600s. Larger move-up and semi-custom homes on premium lots, or homes in higher-end master-planned communities, can reach the $700s and beyond. Keep in mind that base prices rarely reflect the final number: lot premiums, structural options, and design-center selections add up quickly. Having buyer representation helps you understand the true all-in cost before you commit.

Who New Construction Is Right For

New construction suits buyers who want a home that's move-in ready in every sense — nothing to renovate, nothing to update, and no deferred maintenance to inherit. First-time buyers like the predictable pricing and warranty protection. Growing families are drawn to the modern layouts, flexible spaces, and amenity-rich communities. Move-up buyers appreciate the chance to build to their taste, and busy professionals value the low upkeep of a brand-new home. It's also a strong fit for anyone who wants energy-efficient systems and current design without a bidding war on a resale home. The main considerations are timeline — a to-be-built home can take months — and choosing the right community and builder, which is exactly where an experienced buyer's agent earns their keep.

Frequently Asked Questions — New Construction Communities

Where are the new construction communities near Indianapolis?

New construction is concentrated in the growth suburbs — Westfield, Noblesville, Fishers, McCordsville, Whitestown, Bargersville, and Brownsburg — where master-planned and builder communities are opening steadily. These outer-ring areas have the available land for large new neighborhoods.

How much do new construction homes cost in Central Indiana?

New construction homes generally range from the $300s for townhomes and entry-level single-family plans to the $700s and above for larger move-up and semi-custom homes. Most builder communities in the growth suburbs fall in the $350s to $600s, depending on the builder, community, and included features. Contact Your Realty Link for guidance.

Do I need my own agent to buy new construction?

Yes — it's strongly recommended. The on-site sales representative works for the builder, not for you. Having your own buyer's agent at no cost to you means someone is negotiating price and incentives, reviewing the contract, and protecting your interests through construction and closing.
