Here's Your Home Buyer Checklist

Thank you! Your checklist is ready below. Bookmark this page or print it so you can check off each step as you go. We'll also send a copy to your email.

1

Get Ready

Months Before You Search
  1. 1

    Check your credit score

    Aim for 620 or higher for a conventional loan, or 580 or higher for FHA. Pull your free report at AnnualCreditReport.com. If your score needs work, a few months of on-time payments and paying down balances can make a real difference. Don't open new credit cards or take on new debt during this time.

  2. 2

    Calculate your budget

    Use the 28/36 rule: your monthly housing costs (mortgage, taxes, insurance, HOA) should stay under 28% of your gross monthly income, and your total debt payments under 36%. Factor in Indiana property taxes, homeowner's insurance, and any HOA dues for the neighborhoods you're considering.

  3. 3

    Save for your down payment

    FHA loans require 3.5% down, conventional loans start at 3-5% (20% avoids PMI), and VA and USDA loans offer 0% down for eligible buyers. On a $250,000 home, 3.5% down is $8,750. Also save for closing costs (2-4% of the price) and a reserve fund of 2-3 months of housing expenses.

  4. 4

    Research down payment assistance programs

    Indiana IHCDA offers programs like the Next Home and First Home programs with down payment assistance for qualifying buyers. Some counties and cities have additional grants. Your lender or agent can help you identify programs you qualify for — don't skip this step, as many first-time buyers leave free money on the table.

  5. 5

    Get pre-approved (not just pre-qualified)

    Pre-qualification is an estimate. Pre-approval means the lender has verified your income, assets, and credit, and is ready to lend you a specific amount. In Central Indiana's competitive market, sellers take pre-approved offers more seriously. Pre-approval also locks in your interest rate for a set period — usually 60-90 days.

2

Start Your Search

Weeks 1-4
  1. 6

    Hire a buyer's agent

    In Indiana, buyer representation protects your interests throughout the purchase. Since the 2024 industry changes, you'll sign a Buyer Compensation Agreement that clearly spells out your agent's services and how they're paid. Interview 2-3 agents — ask about their experience with first-time buyers, their knowledge of your target neighborhoods, and how they communicate. A good agent saves you money and stress.

  2. 7

    Define your must-haves vs. nice-to-haves

    Write two lists. Must-haves are non-negotiable: number of bedrooms, school district, maximum commute time, garage, single-story, fenced yard. Nice-to-haves are things you'd love but can live without: granite counters, finished basement, updated bathrooms. Being honest about this upfront prevents decision fatigue later.

  3. 8

    Tour homes strategically

    Plan to see 8-12 homes before making a decision. More than that and you'll start second-guessing yourself; fewer and you might not have a good sense of the market. Visit your top choices at different times of day — a quiet street at 10am might be a highway at 5pm. Take photos and notes; after home #6, they all blend together.

  4. 9

    Research the neighborhood

    Look beyond the house itself. Check school ratings, flood zone maps (FEMA), and any HOA rules or restrictions. Search the county assessor site for property tax history. Drive the area on a weekday evening. Ask your agent about future development plans — a proposed highway or commercial project can help or hurt resale value.

3

Make an Offer

Weeks 4-6
  1. 10

    Write a competitive offer with your agent

    Your agent will pull comparable sales and help you determine a fair offer price. You'll also decide on earnest money (typically 1-2% in Central Indiana), contingencies (inspection, appraisal, financing), and a closing timeline. In a competitive situation, your agent can advise on strategies to strengthen your offer without taking unnecessary risks.

  2. 11

    Negotiate

    Your agent handles counteroffers, repair requests, and credit negotiations on your behalf. Most Indiana transactions involve some back-and-forth. Stay focused on what matters most to you — price, repairs, closing date, included appliances — and let your agent manage the conversation. This is one of the biggest reasons to have professional representation.

  3. 12

    Schedule the home inspection

    Budget $350-$500 for a general inspection. Attend it in person — a good inspector will walk you through the house and explain what they find. This is your chance to learn about the home's systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roof). If significant issues surface, your agent can negotiate repairs or credits with the seller.

  4. 13

    Review the appraisal

    Your lender orders an independent appraisal to verify the home's value. If it comes in at or above your offer price, you're in good shape. If it comes in low, your agent negotiates — the seller may lower the price, you may bring additional cash to close the gap, or you may meet somewhere in the middle. Your agent will advise on the best path forward.

4

Close the Deal

Weeks 6-8
  1. 14

    Final walkthrough

    Usually done the day before or morning of closing. Verify that any agreed-upon repairs were completed, nothing has changed since your last visit, all appliances and fixtures that were included are still there, and the home is in the condition you expected. If something's off, your agent addresses it before you sign.

  2. 15

    Closing day

    Bring your government-issued photo ID and certified funds (a cashier's check or wire transfer for your down payment and closing costs — your title company will provide the exact amount in advance). You'll sign the final paperwork, the deed transfers, and the keys are yours. The entire signing usually takes 45-60 minutes. Congratulations — you're a homeowner!

Bonus: Indianapolis-Specific Tips

  • ◆ Indiana is an attorney-optional closing state — you don't need a real estate attorney for a standard closing, but you can hire one if you'd like additional review of your documents. Most buyers in Central Indiana close with a title company only.
  • ◆ Earnest money in Central Indiana is typically 1-2% of the purchase price. On a $275,000 home, expect to put down $2,750-$5,500 in earnest money, which goes toward your down payment at closing.
  • ◆ MIBOR MLS is the listing service for Central Indiana — your buyer's agent has full access to every active listing, including details that public sites like Zillow often miss or delay. This is one of the key advantages of working with a local agent.
  • ◆ Indiana property taxes are capped at 1% for primary residences thanks to the homestead exemption. File your homestead exemption with the county assessor after closing — it's free and it caps your property tax bill. Don't forget this step.
  • ◆ Closing costs for buyers in Indiana typically run 2-4% of the purchase price. This covers title insurance, lender fees, prepaid taxes, homeowner's insurance, and recording fees. Your lender will provide a Loan Estimate within three days of your application so you know what to expect.
