Why Homeowners Fall Behind — and Why It's More Common Than You Think

There is no single reason people fall behind on mortgage payments. Life happens — and sometimes it happens all at once. You are not alone in this, and there is nothing to be ashamed of. Some of the most common situations we see include:

Job Loss or Income Reduction

A layoff, reduced hours, or loss of a second income can make mortgage payments unmanageable — even temporarily.

Medical Expenses

An illness, injury, or ongoing medical costs can consume savings and make it impossible to keep up with housing payments.

Divorce or Separation

When a household splits, the financial picture changes drastically. A mortgage that two incomes supported may not be sustainable on one.

Adjustable Rate Increases

If your mortgage has an adjustable rate, a significant rate increase can push your monthly payment beyond what your budget can handle.

Whatever brought you here, the important thing is that you're looking for answers. That's the first step — and it's a courageous one.

Frequently Asked Questions — Behind on Payments

What happens if I fall behind on my mortgage in Indiana?

If you miss mortgage payments in Indiana, your lender will typically send notices and attempt to work with you. After continued missed payments, they may begin the foreclosure process. Indiana is a judicial foreclosure state, which means the lender must go through the courts — a process that takes several months. This gives you time to explore options like loan modification, forbearance, or selling the home before foreclosure is finalized.

Can I sell my home if I'm behind on payments?

Yes. As long as you have equity in your home — meaning it's worth more than what you owe — you can sell the property, pay off the mortgage from the proceeds, and avoid foreclosure entirely. Even if you owe more than the home is worth, a short sale may be an option with your lender's approval. Selling is often the best way to protect your credit and walk away with cash in hand.
