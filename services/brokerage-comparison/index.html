What Agents Should Actually Evaluate

Most brokerage comparison conversations start and end with the commission split — and that's a mistake. The split matters, obviously, but it's only one variable in a more complex equation. An agent keeping 90 percent of a commission they almost lost because their broker was unreachable during a tough negotiation isn't better off than an agent keeping 80 percent with a broker who helped them save the deal. Here's what genuinely matters when you're evaluating where to work: total cost of being there (splits plus all fees — desk fees, technology fees, franchise fees, E&O, transaction fees), the quality and availability of mentoring and deal support, the tools and technology provided versus what you have to pay for yourself, the culture and whether agents actually collaborate or compete internally, and whether the brokerage has a reputation that helps or hinders your ability to win listings and attract clients.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for in a real estate brokerage?

Focus on five things: commission split and fee structure (what you actually take home), training and mentoring quality (especially if you are newer), technology and lead generation tools provided, the culture and support environment, and the broker's accessibility when you need help on a deal. A brokerage that charges low fees but provides no support may cost you more in lost deals than one with a slightly different split that actually helps you close.

How do commission splits work at a real estate brokerage?

Commission splits determine how the gross commission from a transaction is divided between the agent and the brokerage. Splits vary widely — some brokerages offer high splits with monthly desk fees, others offer lower splits with no fees, and some use tiered or cap structures. The right split depends on your production level, the services included, and what you actually need from your brokerage. Your Realty Link offers competitive splits with no monthly fees — details are discussed during a confidential conversation.

Does Your Realty Link charge monthly fees?

No. Your Realty Link does not charge monthly desk fees, franchise fees, or technology fees. Our agents keep more of what they earn because our model is designed around supporting agents without nickel-and-diming them. We believe a brokerage should make money when its agents make money — not from collecting recurring fees regardless of production.

Can I switch brokerages mid-transaction?

Generally, active transactions stay with the brokerage where they originated, and you would complete those deals under your current broker before transferring your license. The timing of a move depends on your current agreements and pipeline. Your Realty Link is happy to discuss the logistics of transitioning in a confidential conversation — we have helped many agents make smooth moves from other brokerages.
