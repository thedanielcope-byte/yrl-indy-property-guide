What Buyer Representation Really Means

Buyer representation isn't just having someone open doors at showings. A true buyer's agent is your advocate from the moment you start searching to the day you receive your keys — and every stressful, confusing step in between. When you work with a listing agent directly (the agent who represents the seller), that agent has a legal obligation to the seller, not to you. They cannot advise you on what to offer, flag problems they're aware of, or negotiate against their own client. A buyer's agent works exclusively for you. That means your interests, your budget, and your goals drive every decision. Frequently Asked Questions

Does buyer representation cost me anything in Indianapolis?

In most transactions, the seller pays the buyer's agent commission as part of the sale. This means you get full professional representation — from home search through closing — at no direct cost to you. Your Realty Link will explain exactly how compensation works before you sign anything, so there are no surprises.

What does a buyer's agent actually do?

A buyer's agent helps you find homes that match your criteria, schedules and attends showings, writes and negotiates offers on your behalf, coordinates inspections, reviews contracts, and guides you through closing. They represent your interests only — not the seller's — which is a critical distinction in every transaction.

Can I search homes without a buyer's agent?

You can browse listings online, but to tour homes listed on the MIBOR MLS and have someone negotiate on your behalf, you need a licensed agent. Without representation, you're navigating one of the largest financial decisions of your life alone — and the listing agent represents the seller, not you.
