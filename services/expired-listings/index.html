If your home sat on the market for weeks or months and didn't sell, you're not alone — and you're not out of options. An expired listing is one of the most discouraging experiences a homeowner can go through. You prepared your home, cooperated with showings, and trusted the process — and it still didn't work. That's a real disappointment, and it's okay to feel that way. But here's what's important to understand: in most cases, an expired listing is a strategy problem, not a home problem. Your Realty Link has helped many Central Indiana homeowners successfully relist and sell homes that previously expired, and we're ready to do the same for you.

When a Listing Expires, It Can Feel Personal

Most homeowners whose listings expire wonder the same things: Was it my home? Did I price it wrong? Was my agent working hard enough? Should I just give up? These are completely natural questions. The truth is that most expired listings come down to one or more of these core issues:

Overpricing

The most common reason. Buyers and their agents are well-informed — if a home is priced above market, it simply gets skipped.

Weak Marketing

Poor photos, minimal online presence, or no targeted outreach means the right buyers never see the home at all.

Presentation Issues

Small updates — decluttering, staging, curb appeal — can dramatically shift buyer perception and willingness to make an offer.

Timing & Strategy

Market conditions shift. A home listed at the wrong time or with the wrong positioning can sit even when demand is present.

None of these issues mean your home can't sell. They mean the approach needs to change — and that's exactly where Your Realty Link comes in.

Frequently Asked Questions — Expired Listings

Why did my listing expire without selling?

Most expired listings come down to overpricing, limited marketing exposure, weak listing photos, or a mismatch between the agent's approach and what the market requires. An expired listing is not a reflection of your home — it's a signal that the strategy needs to change.

Can I relist my home after it expires?

Yes. Once your listing agreement expires, you are free to work with a new agent or relist with the same one. Your Realty Link recommends a fresh market analysis, updated pricing, and a renewed marketing strategy before relisting to generate maximum buyer interest.

How soon can Your Realty Link relist my home?

We can typically begin the process within 24–48 hours of your initial consultation. After reviewing your home and the current market, we'll prepare an updated pricing strategy, gather fresh photography if needed, and launch your relisting on the MIBOR MLS with a full marketing campaign.

Does an expired listing hurt my chances of selling?

Not necessarily. Many homes that didn't sell the first time go on to sell quickly once relisted with the right price, presentation, and marketing. Buyers do pay attention to days on market, so a strategic relaunch — sometimes including a brief off-market period to reset the listing clock — can help restore buyer interest and urgency.
