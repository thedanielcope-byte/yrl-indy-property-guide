What First-Time Buyers Are Really Going Through

Here's the honest truth: buying your first home involves a lot of unknowns. You may not know what you can actually afford versus what a lender will approve you for. You might worry about choosing the wrong neighborhood, overpaying, or missing something important in a home inspection. You've probably heard horror stories — bidding wars, hidden problems, deals falling through. Those things happen, but they happen far less often when you have an experienced agent guiding you. Most first-time buyers also don't fully understand all the costs involved: down payment, closing costs, property taxes, homeowner's insurance, and possibly HOA dues. Getting clear on the full picture before you start shopping is one of the most important things you can do — and that's where we start.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do I need for a down payment as a first-time buyer in Indiana?

It depends on your loan type. FHA loans require as little as 3.5% down. Conventional loans can go as low as 3% down for qualified buyers. VA and USDA loans may require zero down payment for eligible borrowers. Indiana also has down payment assistance programs through IHCDA that can help cover upfront costs. Your Realty Link can connect you with local lenders who specialize in first-time buyer programs.

What credit score do I need to buy a home in Indianapolis?

Most conventional loans require a credit score of 620 or higher. FHA loans can be approved with scores in the mid-500s with a larger down payment. The higher your score, the better the interest rate you'll typically qualify for. If your credit needs work, a good lender can walk you through a plan to improve your score before applying.

How long does it take to buy a home in Indianapolis?

From pre-approval to closing, the typical timeline is 30 to 60 days once you have an accepted offer. Finding the right home varies — some buyers find it in a weekend, others take a few months. The more clarity you have on what you want and the more prepared you are financially, the faster the process tends to move.

Do I need a buyer's agent as a first-time home buyer?

Absolutely. As a first-time buyer, having a buyer's agent is even more important than for experienced buyers. Your agent explains every step, helps you avoid costly mistakes, writes your offers, negotiates on your behalf, and makes sure you understand what you're signing. And in most transactions, the seller covers your agent's commission — so it costs you nothing.
