Few markets in the Midwest offer the fix-and-flip potential of Indianapolis. Relatively affordable entry prices, a deep inventory of older homes ripe for renovation, and steady buyer demand have made Central Indiana a magnet for investors — from first-time flippers to seasoned operators scaling a portfolio. But profitable flipping isn't about luck; it's about discipline. The money is made when you buy, not when you sell, and the difference between a winning project and a costly mistake usually comes down to accurate analysis and a strong resale strategy. Your Realty Link works with investors on both ends of the deal — sourcing and evaluating the purchase, then marketing the finished home to sell fast and for the best price.

Why Investors Flip in Indianapolis

The fundamentals that draw flippers to Indianapolis have held up well: affordability, demand, and supply of renovation candidates. For investors who buy carefully and control costs, the metro offers a workable spread between acquisition cost and after-repair value.

Affordable Entry

Indianapolis acquisition prices remain accessible compared with many large metros, lowering the capital barrier to a deal.

Renovation Inventory

A large stock of older and distressed homes provides a steady pipeline of properties with upside.

Steady Resale Demand

Strong, consistent buyer demand across price points helps finished flips move when priced right.

Diverse Neighborhoods

From east-side bungalows to suburban ranches, the metro offers flip opportunities across many submarkets.

Frequently Asked Questions — Fix and Flip

Is Indianapolis a good market for fix and flip?

Indianapolis has long attracted flippers thanks to its relatively affordable entry prices, steady buyer demand, and large inventory of older homes that benefit from renovation. As with any market, success comes down to buying right, controlling rehab costs, and pricing the resale accurately — areas where a knowledgeable local agent adds real value.

How do I find fix and flip properties in Indianapolis?

Deals come from the MLS, off-market sources, distressed and estate sales, and agent networks. Your Realty Link helps investors identify candidates that fit their budget and strategy, and runs the numbers so you know a property's potential before you commit.

What is the 70% rule in flipping?

The 70% rule is a common guideline suggesting an investor pay no more than 70% of a property's after-repair value (ARV) minus estimated rehab costs. It's a starting point, not a guarantee — every deal requires careful, property-specific analysis of comps, repairs, holding costs, and resale pricing.

Can Your Realty Link help me sell my flip?

Yes. We help investors on both ends — sourcing and analyzing the purchase, then listing and marketing the finished home on the MIBOR MLS to resell quickly and for top dollar.
