What the FSBO Reality Looks Like for Most Sellers

Research from the National Association of Real Estate BrokerS® consistently shows that FSBO homes sell for significantly less than agent-listed homes — in many studies, the gap is large enough to wipe out the commission savings entirely. That doesn't mean FSBO can't work, but it does mean the math isn't as simple as "no agent = more money."

Here's what FSBO sellers in Indianapolis typically run into. First, without MIBOR MLS access, your home's exposure is sharply limited. Most buyers work with agents who search the MLS — if your home isn't there, a large portion of the buying pool won't even know it exists. Second, pricing without professional data is genuinely difficult. Overpricing leads to a stale listing; underpricing costs you money at closing. Third, Indiana real estate contracts are detailed legal documents. Errors or missing disclosures can create post-closing liability. Fourth, qualifying buyers, navigating inspections, and managing the closing process takes time and expertise that most sellers underestimate before they're in the middle of it.

None of this is meant to discourage you — it's meant to help you go in with open eyes.

FSBO Frequently Asked Questions

Do FSBO homes sell for less than agent-listed homes?

Research consistently shows that FSBO homes sell for less on average than agent-represented homes. The gap is large enough that in most cases the commission savings are more than offset by the lower sale price — meaning sellers who go FSBO often net less money, not more.

What are the biggest risks of selling FSBO in Indianapolis?

The biggest risks include underpricing or overpricing the home, limited exposure without MLS access, contract errors that can expose you to legal liability, and difficulty qualifying buyers. Indiana's disclosure requirements are also detailed — mistakes can result in post-closing disputes.

Can I list on the MLS without an agent?

Access to the MIBOR MLS is limited to licensed REALTORS®. Some flat-fee services offer MLS entry for a fee, but they provide no representation, no pricing guidance, no contract help, and no negotiation support. You get MLS visibility but handle everything else yourself.
