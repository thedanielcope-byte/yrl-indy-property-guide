Frequently Asked Questions

Is a CMA the same as a home appraisal?

No — a CMA (Comparative Market Analysis) and a formal appraisal are different. A CMA is prepared by a licensed real estate agent using recent comparable sales and market data to estimate a home's likely sale price. An appraisal is conducted by a licensed appraiser and is typically required by lenders to confirm the value of a home being financed. CMAs are free and used for pricing decisions; appraisals are paid services used in the mortgage process.

How accurate are online home value estimates like Zillow's Zestimate?

Online estimates can be a useful starting point, but they're often significantly off — sometimes by tens of thousands of dollars. They rely on public records and algorithms that can't account for your home's specific condition, recent updates, location nuances within a neighborhood, or current local market dynamics. A professional CMA from a MIBOR agent who knows your area is far more accurate and useful for making real decisions.

Who should get a free home valuation?

Anyone who owns a home in Central Indiana can benefit from knowing what it's worth. The most common reasons homeowners request a CMA include: planning to sell in the near future, curious about current equity, going through a divorce or estate situation, considering a refinance, or simply wanting to understand where they stand financially. A CMA is free and comes with no obligation to list.
