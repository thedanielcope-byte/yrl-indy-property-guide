How Home Equity Works

Home equity is straightforward in concept but powerful in practice. It's the portion of your home that you truly own — the difference between your home's market value and what you owe on your mortgage. Equity builds in two ways, and most homeowners benefit from both.

Mortgage Paydown

Every monthly mortgage payment reduces your loan balance. Over time, more of each payment goes toward principal, and your equity grows steadily through this process alone.

Home Appreciation

When your home's market value increases — due to market demand, neighborhood improvements, or property upgrades — your equity grows even without making extra payments.

Home Improvements

Strategic renovations like kitchen updates, bathroom remodels, or adding living space can increase your home's value and boost your equity position.

Market Conditions

Central Indiana has experienced consistent appreciation across most markets. Homeowners in cities like Fishers, Carmel, and Greenwood have seen particularly strong gains in recent years.

Frequently Asked Questions — Home Equity

What is home equity?

Home equity is the difference between what your home is worth on the open market and what you still owe on your mortgage. For example, if your home is worth $350,000 and you owe $200,000, you have $150,000 in equity. Equity grows as you pay down your mortgage and as your home appreciates in value.

How do I find out how much equity I have?

You need two numbers: your current mortgage balance (found on your loan statement) and your home's current market value. Your Realty Link provides free comparative market analyses that give you a reliable estimate of what your home would sell for today. Contact us for a no-obligation valuation.

Can I access my home equity without selling?

Yes. Homeowners can access equity through a home equity loan, a home equity line of credit (HELOC), or a cash-out refinance. Each option has different terms and implications. We recommend discussing these with a mortgage lender. If you decide selling is the better route, Your Realty Link is here to help.

How has home equity changed in Indianapolis recently?

Central Indiana has seen strong home price appreciation in recent years, meaning many homeowners have significantly more equity than they realize. Even homeowners who purchased within the last several years may have built substantial equity through a combination of mortgage payments and market appreciation.
