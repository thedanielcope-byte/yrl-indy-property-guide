How Commissions Work at YRL

Real estate agents at YRL (like most companies) are independent contractors whose income is set by their own volume of sales. Take a simplified example: if you earn 3% on one side of a transaction and close consistent volume, your annual income is truly a reflection of your effort — not an arbitrary salary cap.

At YRL, agents pay a portion of their commission to the brokerage until they reach the annual cap. Once you hit the cap, you pay zero to YRL until the new year starts. No monthly fees eating into your earnings in the meantime. No surprise deductions. Just a simple, transparent structure that rewards production.

How Much Does It Cost to Get Started?

Getting your license active and joining MIBOR, IAR, and NAR runs roughly $2,500 in your first year — covering state fees and association dues. Where else can you invest that amount in a career with truly unlimited earning potential? Real estate is one of the last industries where your income is genuinely set by your own dedication and effort.
