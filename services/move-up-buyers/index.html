Maybe your family has grown. Maybe your commute has changed. The Move-Up Balancing Act

The biggest challenge for move-up buyers is timing. You love the idea of upgrading, but the logistics of selling and buying simultaneously can feel overwhelming. Here's what most move-up buyers are weighing:

Sell First or Buy First?

Each approach has trade-offs. Selling first means you know your budget but may need temporary housing. Buying first avoids the gap but means two mortgages temporarily.

Equity as Down Payment

Your current home's equity is likely your biggest asset for your next purchase. Knowing exactly how much you'll net is critical before you start shopping.

School Districts & Timing

If you have school-age children, moving between districts often needs to happen during summer. That narrows your window and raises the stakes.

Market Conditions

In a seller's market, your home sells fast but upgrade inventory is tight. In a buyer's market, you have more choices but your sale may take longer. Strategy matters.

Frequently Asked Questions — Move-Up Buyers

Should I sell my current home before buying my next one?

It depends on your financial situation and the market. Selling first gives you a known budget and stronger buying position, but you may need temporary housing. Buying first eliminates the gap but means carrying two mortgages temporarily. Your Realty Link helps you evaluate both approaches and choose the one that works for your situation.

How much equity do I need to move up?

There is no fixed amount. The equity in your current home becomes your down payment on the next one. We provide a free home valuation and net proceeds estimate so you know exactly how much you can put toward your upgrade before you start shopping.

Can Your Realty Link help me buy and sell at the same time?

Yes. Coordinating a simultaneous buy-sell transaction is one of our core services. Daniel Cope manages both sides of your move — pricing and marketing your current home while helping you search for and negotiate on your next one — with aligned timelines to minimize disruption.

What are the best neighborhoods for move-up homes in Indianapolis?

Popular move-up markets include Carmel, Fishers, Zionsville, Westfield, and Noblesville in Hamilton County, as well as Avon, Brownsburg, and Greenwood. The best choice depends on your budget, commute, school preferences, and lifestyle priorities. Contact Your Realty Link for personalized neighborhood recommendations.
