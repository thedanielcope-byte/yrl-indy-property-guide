Types of Off-Market Opportunities

Off-market doesn't mean one thing — there are several types of opportunities that fall outside the traditional MLS listing process:

Pocket Listings

Properties where the seller has signed a listing agreement but the agent markets the home privately through their network before putting it on the MLS.

Pre-Market Properties

Homes being prepared for sale — repairs, staging, photography — where the seller is willing to entertain early offers from serious buyers.

Quiet Sellers

Homeowners who would sell if the right buyer approached them but aren't willing to go through the public listing process. These are found through direct outreach.

Investor Dispositions

Investment properties being sold by other investors — often marketed within investor networks rather than on the open market, especially for bulk or portfolio transactions.

Each type of off-market deal requires a different approach, and the common thread is the same: you need an agent who is well-connected, proactive, and plugged into the local real estate community. That's what Your Realty Link brings to the table.

Frequently Asked Questions — Off-Market Properties

What is an off-market property?

An off-market property is a home or investment property available for sale that is not publicly listed on the MLS or major real estate websites. These include pocket listings, pre-market properties, and homes where the seller prefers a private sale. Off-market deals are typically found through agent networks and direct relationships.

How do I find off-market homes in Indianapolis?

The best way to access off-market properties in Indianapolis is to work with an agent who has strong local connections. Your Realty Link maintains relationships with other MIBOR agents, investors, and property owners across Central Indiana, giving our clients access to opportunities before they hit the public market.

Are off-market deals better than MLS listings?

Not always — but they can be. Off-market properties often have less buyer competition, which can result in better negotiating leverage and more favorable terms. However, they also require more due diligence since there may be less publicly available information. Working with a knowledgeable local agent is essential to ensure you're getting a fair deal.
