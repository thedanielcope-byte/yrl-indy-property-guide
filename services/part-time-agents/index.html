Real estate doesn't have to be all or nothing. Plenty of successful agents in Central Indiana started part time — working evenings and weekends around a full-time job, building their client base gradually, and growing into the career on their own terms. The problem is that most brokerages aren't set up for that. They charge desk fees that eat into your earnings before you close a single deal. They require minimum production that punishes agents who are still building. And they offer training schedules designed for people with nothing else on their calendar. Your Realty Link is different. We believe that a part-time agent who's dedicated, honest, and willing to learn deserves the same support and respect as someone doing this full time. If you have your Indiana real estate license — or you're working on getting it — and you want a brokerage that actually fits the way you work, we'd like to talk to you.

Why Agents Choose to Work Part Time

There are many good reasons to start — or stay — part time in real estate. Here are some of the most common situations we see at Your Realty Link:

Career Transition

You're testing real estate while keeping the stability of your current income. Smart move — it lets you build skills and a client base before making a full commitment.

Supplemental Income

You enjoy real estate and want to earn additional income from it — a few deals a year alongside your primary career can add up significantly.

Family Obligations

You're a parent, caregiver, or student who needs flexibility. Real estate lets you work around your life instead of the other way around.

Newly Licensed

You just passed your exam and want to gain experience without the financial pressure of going full time on day one. Part-time is a natural starting point.

Whatever your reason, Your Realty Link doesn't treat part-time agents as second-class. You get the same tools, the same support, and the same access as every other agent at the brokerage.

Frequently Asked Questions — Part-Time Agents

Can I be a part-time real estate agent in Indiana?

Yes. Indiana does not require real estate agents to work full time. As long as you hold an active Indiana real estate license and are affiliated with a brokerage, you can practice part time. Your Realty Link actively supports part-time agents with the tools and flexibility they need to succeed.

How many deals can a part-time agent realistically close?

Most part-time agents at Your Realty Link close between 3 and 10 transactions per year, depending on their sphere of influence, time investment, and lead generation activity. Even a few deals per year can generate meaningful supplemental income alongside a primary career.

Does Your Realty Link charge desk fees or monthly minimums?

Your Realty Link offers a competitive commission structure without burdensome desk fees or monthly production minimums that penalize part-time agents. Contact us for specific details about our current compensation plans.

What support does a part-time agent get at Your Realty Link?

Part-time agents receive the same support as full-time agents: MIBOR MLS access, transaction coordination assistance, marketing tools, training resources, mentorship from experienced agents, and direct access to broker Daniel Cope for questions and guidance.
