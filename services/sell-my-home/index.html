What Sellers Are Really Dealing With

Whether you've lived in your home for five years or twenty-five, selling it involves a mix of financial calculation and genuine emotion — and that combination can lead to decisions that cost money if you're not careful. Overpricing is the most common and most costly mistake sellers make, and it's often driven by a mix of optimism and attachment to a home's personal value rather than its market value. Underinvesting in presentation — skipping professional photos, leaving obvious deferred maintenance, failing to depersonalize — costs sellers money too. And working with the wrong agent, or no agent at all, means navigating complex contracts, disclosure requirements, inspections, and negotiations without professional guidance on a transaction where every detail matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know what my home is worth in Indianapolis?

The most accurate way is a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) from a local MIBOR agent. A CMA looks at recent sales of similar homes in your area, current active listings (your competition), and market trend data to arrive at a well-supported price range. Online estimates like Zestimates are a starting point but are often significantly off because they can't account for your home's specific condition, upgrades, and location nuances. Your Realty Link provides free CMAs with no obligation.

We offer competitive, full-service listing representation. Commission structure is discussed during your listing consultation — we believe in transparent conversations about what you'll pay and what you'll get in return. We don't cut corners on service, and we don't think sellers should have to choose between getting great representation and keeping more of their proceeds.

How long does it take to sell a home in Indianapolis?

It depends on the home, the price, and current market conditions. Well-priced homes in good condition in desirable areas often receive offers within the first one to two weeks on the market. Homes that are overpriced or need significant updates may take longer. Your Realty Link will give you a realistic expectation based on your specific home and neighborhood — not a number designed to make you feel good in the moment.

Should I make repairs before listing my Indianapolis home?

It depends on the repairs and the market. Some updates — fresh paint, deep cleaning, minor fixes, improved curb appeal — almost always pay off. Major renovations are more complex and don't always return their cost. During your listing consultation, we'll walk through your home and give you honest, practical recommendations on what's worth doing before you list and what you can skip.
