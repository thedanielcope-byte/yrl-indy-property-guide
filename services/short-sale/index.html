Falling behind on your mortgage, or owing more than your home is worth, is one of the most stressful situations a homeowner can face. Maybe it followed a job loss, a divorce, a medical hardship, or simply a market shift — and now you're worried about what comes next. First, take a breath. You are not alone, and a foreclosure is not your only path forward. A short sale can offer a more controlled, less damaging way to move on. Your Realty Link has helped Indianapolis-area homeowners through difficult transitions with patience and discretion, and we're here to help you understand your options without pressure or judgment.

You're Facing a Hard Situation — Let's Talk Through It

When you're underwater on your mortgage or struggling to keep up with payments, it's easy to feel stuck. Many homeowners in this position share the same worries: Will I lose my home? What happens to my credit? Can I even sell if I owe too much? Is anyone going to treat me with respect through this? These concerns are completely valid. A short sale — selling your home for less than you owe, with your lender's approval — exists precisely for situations like these.

An Alternative to Foreclosure

A short sale lets you sell on your terms rather than going through the foreclosure process.

Often Gentler on Credit

For many homeowners, a short sale is less damaging to credit than a foreclosure on their record.

More Control & Dignity

You stay involved in the process and work toward a resolution instead of having one imposed on you.

A Path to a Fresh Start

Resolving the situation lets you close this chapter and begin rebuilding with peace of mind.

A short sale isn't the right answer for everyone, and there are financial, tax, and legal considerations worth discussing with the right professionals. But for many homeowners, it's a far better outcome than waiting for foreclosure — and that's a conversation worth having.

Frequently Asked Questions — Short Sales

What is a short sale?

A short sale is when you sell your home for less than the amount owed on the mortgage, with your lender's approval to accept the reduced payoff. It's an alternative to foreclosure that can help homeowners move on while limiting damage to their credit and finances.

Is a short sale better than foreclosure?

For many homeowners, yes. A short sale is generally less damaging to your credit than a foreclosure and gives you more control over the process. Every situation is different, so it's wise to consult financial, tax, and legal professionals about your specific circumstances.

How long does a short sale take in Indiana?

Short sales typically take longer than a standard sale because the lender must approve the reduced payoff. Timelines vary depending on the lender and complexity, often ranging from a couple of months to longer. Your Realty Link helps keep the process moving and keeps you informed at every step.

Do I need an agent for a short sale?

It's strongly recommended. Short sales involve lender negotiation, documentation, and coordination that a standard sale doesn't. An experienced agent helps you navigate the paperwork and the lender process so you can focus on moving forward.
