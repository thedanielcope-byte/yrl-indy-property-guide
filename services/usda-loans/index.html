One of the biggest barriers to homeownership is the down payment — and for many buyers in Central Indiana, that barrier doesn't have to exist. The USDA Rural Development Guaranteed Housing Loan Program offers 100 percent financing with no down payment required for eligible buyers purchasing homes in qualifying rural areas. The catch? Most people assume "rural" means living an hour from the nearest grocery store. In reality, many communities within a comfortable commute of Indianapolis qualify for USDA financing — places like Shelbyville, Mooresville, and parts of Hancock, Boone, Madison, and southern Johnson counties. Your Realty Link works with first-time buyers and experienced buyers alike to identify USDA-eligible properties and connect them with lenders who specialize in this program.

What Makes USDA Loans Different

USDA loans are backed by the United States Department of Agriculture and designed to promote homeownership in rural and suburban communities. They're one of the most buyer-friendly loan programs available in Indiana, but they come with specific eligibility requirements that most conventional or FHA loans don't have. The property must be in a USDA-eligible area, and the buyer's household income must fall within the program's limits for that county. Understanding these requirements upfront saves time and frustration — and that's where working with an agent who knows the USDA landscape in Central Indiana makes a real difference.

Zero Down Payment

The defining feature of USDA loans. You can finance 100 percent of the home's purchase price — no down payment required. This makes homeownership accessible to buyers who have good income but haven't saved a large lump sum.

Lower Mortgage Insurance

USDA guarantee fees are typically lower than FHA mortgage insurance premiums or PMI on conventional loans, which translates to a lower monthly payment for the same home price.

Flexible Credit Requirements

While USDA loans aren't no-credit-check loans, they do accommodate buyers with credit scores that might not qualify for the best conventional rates. Many lenders approve USDA loans with scores in the low 600s.

Competitive Interest Rates

Because USDA loans are government-backed, lenders can offer competitive interest rates — often comparable to or better than conventional loan rates for similar borrower profiles.

Frequently Asked Questions

What areas near Indianapolis qualify for USDA loans?

USDA eligibility is determined by population density, not just whether an area looks rural. Many areas within commuting distance of Indianapolis qualify, including much of Shelby County, Morgan County, parts of Hancock County, rural Boone County, southern Johnson County, and portions of Madison County. Eligibility can change, so it is important to verify specific addresses using the USDA eligibility map or by working with an agent who knows which areas qualify.

What are the income limits for USDA loans in Indiana?

USDA loans have household income limits that vary by county and household size. Generally, your total household income cannot exceed 115 percent of the area median income for the county where the home is located. For many counties near Indianapolis, this means a household of one to four people can earn a moderate income and still qualify. Income limits are updated periodically, so check with a USDA-approved lender for current figures.

Can I use a USDA loan to buy an existing home or only new construction?

USDA loans can be used for both existing homes and new construction, as long as the property is in an eligible rural area and will be your primary residence. The home must meet minimum property standards, but the program is not limited to new builds. Many buyers use USDA loans to purchase existing single-family homes in small towns and rural communities near Indianapolis.

How does a USDA loan compare to an FHA loan?

The biggest difference is the down payment. USDA loans require zero down payment, while FHA loans require a minimum of 3.5 percent. USDA loans also tend to have lower mortgage insurance costs. However, USDA loans are restricted to eligible rural areas and have household income limits, while FHA loans can be used anywhere and have no income cap. Your Realty Link can help you evaluate which program fits your situation best.
