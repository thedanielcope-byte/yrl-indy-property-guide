What the VA Loan Benefit Offers Indiana Buyers

The VA loan program is backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs and offered through private lenders — meaning VA loans are available from most banks and mortgage companies, but with significant protections and benefits for eligible borrowers. The most talked-about benefit is zero down payment, which allows veterans to purchase a home without saving tens of thousands of dollars upfront. But that's just the start. VA loans don't require private mortgage insurance (PMI), which can save hundreds of dollars per month compared to conventional loans with low down payments. VA loans also have competitive interest rates — often lower than conventional options — and limit the closing costs that buyers can be charged. There is a VA funding fee, which helps sustain the program, but many veterans with service-connected disabilities are exempt from paying it, and it can typically be rolled into the loan amount for those who owe it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for a VA loan in Indiana?

VA loans are available to eligible veterans, active-duty service members, National Guard and Reserve members (with qualifying service), and surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability. To use your VA benefit, you'll need a Certificate of Eligibility (COE), which your lender can typically obtain directly from the VA.

Do VA loans require a down payment?

In most cases, no. VA loans allow eligible borrowers to purchase a home with zero down payment — one of the most significant benefits the program offers. There is a VA funding fee that is typically rolled into the loan, though some veterans with service-connected disabilities may be exempt from this fee.

Are there homes that don't qualify for VA financing?

VA loans have Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs) similar to FHA standards. The home must be safe, structurally sound, and sanitary. Homes with major deferred maintenance, significant structural issues, or certain safety hazards may require repairs before a VA loan can close. Working with an agent experienced in VA transactions helps you identify strong candidates from the start.

Can I use my VA benefit more than once?

Yes. Your VA entitlement can be restored after you sell a home purchased with a VA loan and pay it off — or in some cases you can have multiple VA loans simultaneously. A VA-experienced lender can review your current entitlement status and explain your options based on your specific situation.
