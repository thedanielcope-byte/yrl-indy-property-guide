Owning a vacant home is stressful in ways people don't talk about enough. Maybe you've already moved, maybe you inherited a property, maybe your tenants moved out and you'd rather sell than re-rent. Whatever the reason, an empty house sitting on the market brings concerns that occupied homes don't face — security risks, maintenance issues, insurance gaps, and the simple reality that empty rooms don't photograph or show as well. If you're trying to sell a vacant home in Indianapolis or anywhere in Central Indiana, Your Realty Link can help. Why Vacant Homes Need a Different Approach

Selling a vacant property isn't the same as selling a lived-in home. Empty houses present a specific set of challenges that, if ignored, can lead to longer time on market and lower offers.

Buyer Perception

Empty rooms look smaller. Without furniture, buyers struggle to envision how they'd live in the space. This affects both showings and online listing photos.

Security Concerns

Vacant homes are targets for vandalism, theft, and squatters. The longer a home sits empty, the greater the risk — especially in certain neighborhoods.

Maintenance & Upkeep

No one is there to notice a leak, a broken window, or a furnace that quit. Small problems become expensive problems in an empty house.

Insurance Gaps

Many standard homeowner policies limit coverage after a home has been vacant for 30 to 60 days. You may need a separate vacant home policy to stay protected.

These are solvable problems, but they require an agent who recognizes them upfront and builds a plan around them. That's exactly what Your Realty Link does.

How Your Realty Link Sells Vacant Properties

When you list a vacant home with Your Realty Link, we don't just put it on the MIBOR MLS and hope for the best. We take an active approach that addresses the specific challenges of selling an empty property:

  • Staging strategy — Depending on your budget and price point, we'll recommend full staging, partial staging, or virtual staging to help buyers connect with the space. Even a few key pieces in the living room and primary bedroom can dramatically improve buyer response.

  • Aggressive pricing and marketing — Vacant homes benefit from being priced competitively from day one. We combine accurate market data with targeted digital marketing to drive early interest and offers.

Frequently Asked Questions — Vacant Home Sales

Is it harder to sell a vacant home?

Vacant homes can sit longer because empty rooms feel smaller and less inviting to buyers. However, with the right staging strategy and pricing, vacant homes sell regularly across Central Indiana. Your Realty Link uses virtual staging and targeted marketing to help buyers see the potential.

Do I need to stage an empty house?

Not always, but staging — even partial or virtual staging — significantly improves buyer response. We'll evaluate your home and recommend the approach that makes the most sense for your price point and market conditions.

What about insurance on a vacant home?

Most standard homeowner policies have limitations or exclusions for homes left vacant beyond 30 to 60 days. We recommend contacting your insurance provider to discuss a vacant home policy. We can also recommend security measures to reduce your risk.

How does Your Realty Link protect my vacant property during showings?

We use electronic lockboxes that log every agent access, coordinate all showings through a central scheduling system, and conduct regular check-ins on the property. You'll know exactly who enters your home and when.
